Every year, millions of farmers across India receive ₹6,000 directly in their bank accounts through **PM Kisan Samman Nidhi** — no middlemen, no paperwork after registration, no trips to a government office. The money just arrives.

But here's the thing — many eligible farmers either don't know they qualify, haven't completed the eKYC step, or are confused about why their installment didn't come. If you're a farmer or helping a farmer in your family, this guide covers everything you need to know about PM Kisan in 2026.

---

## What is PM Kisan Samman Nidhi?

**PM Kisan Samman Nidhi** (Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi) is a central government scheme that gives **₹6,000 per year** to small and marginal farmers across India. The money is transferred in **3 equal installments of ₹2,000 each**, directly into the farmer's Aadhaar-linked bank account.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Annual Benefit** | ₹6,000 (₹2,000 × 3 installments) |
| **Transfer Method** | Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) to bank account |
| **Installment Schedule** | April–July, August–November, December–March |
| **Official Website** | pmkisan.gov.in |
| **Launched** | December 2018 |

Since its launch, the scheme has transferred crores of rupees to farmers across every state in India. It's one of the largest direct cash transfer programmes in the world.

## Who is Eligible for PM Kisan?

Before checking your status or applying, make sure you actually qualify. Here's who is eligible:

**You ARE eligible if:**
- You are a small or marginal farmer
- Your family owns cultivable land (any amount — the 2 hectare limit was removed in 2019)
- You have an Aadhaar card
- You have a bank account linked to Aadhaar
- Your name appears in the land records of your state

**You are NOT eligible if:**
- You or your spouse holds a constitutional post (President, Minister, MP, MLA, etc.)
- You are a current or retired government employee (Central or State) drawing a pension above ₹10,000/month
- You are an income tax payer
- You are a professional (doctor, lawyer, CA, engineer) registered with a professional body
- You are a retired officer or employee of Central/State PSUs and autonomous bodies

If you're a regular farmer with land in your name and none of the above applies — you qualify.

## How to Check PM Kisan Payment Status (Step by Step)

If you're already registered and want to check whether your installment has been released or credited:

**Step 1** — Go to the official website: **pmkisan.gov.in**

**Step 2** — On the homepage, look for **"Farmer Corner"** section (usually in the middle or top-right of the page)

**Step 3** — Click on **"Beneficiary Status"**

**Step 4** — Enter any one of the following:
- Aadhaar Number, OR
- Account Number, OR
- Mobile Number

**Step 5** — Click **"Get Data"**

You will see your installment history — which installments were sent, which were credited to your account, and the dates of transfer.

### What the Status Means

| Status Shown | What it Means |
|-------------|---------------|
| **FTO is Generated and Payment Confirmation is Pending** | Money is on its way, not yet in your account |
| **Rft Signed** | File approved, payment will be processed soon |
| **Payment Released** | Money has been sent to your bank |
| **Payment Credited** | Money is in your bank account ✓ |
| **Beneficiary Not Found** | Name not in PM Kisan database — need to register |

## How to Check Your Name in the Beneficiary List

Want to confirm you're in the official list for your village or district?

1. Go to **pmkisan.gov.in**
2. Click **"Farmer Corner"** → **"Beneficiary List"**
3. Select your **State → District → Sub-District → Block → Village**
4. Click **"Get Report"**

A full list of registered farmers in your village will appear. If your name is there, you're registered. If not, you need to apply.

## How to Register for PM Kisan (New Registration)

If you're eligible but not yet registered, here's how to apply:

### Option 1 — Online (Self Registration)

1. Go to **pmkisan.gov.in**
2. Click **"Farmer Corner"** → **"New Farmer Registration"**
3. Choose **Rural Farmer** or **Urban Farmer**
4. Enter your **Aadhaar Number** and **Mobile Number**
5. Complete OTP verification
6. Fill in your personal details — name, father's name, date of birth, gender, category
7. Enter your **bank account details** (account number, IFSC code)
8. Enter your **land details** — survey number, khasra number, area in hectares
9. Submit the form

### Option 2 — Through CSC Centre

If you're not comfortable doing it online, visit your nearest **Common Service Centre (CSC)** or **Gram Panchayat** office. Bring:
- Aadhaar card
- Bank passbook (showing account number and IFSC)
- Land record / Khasra-Khatauni document
- Mobile number linked to Aadhaar

The CSC operator will register you at a nominal charge (usually ₹0 — it's a free service).

## eKYC — Why Your Installment May Be Stuck

This is the most common reason farmers miss their PM Kisan installments in 2026.

**eKYC is mandatory.** If you haven't completed eKYC, your installment will be held even if you're a registered beneficiary. The government made this compulsory to remove fake beneficiaries from the system.

### How to Complete PM Kisan eKYC

**Method 1 — OTP-based (Online)**
1. Go to **pmkisan.gov.in** → **"Farmer Corner"** → **"eKYC"**
2. Enter your Aadhaar number
3. An OTP will be sent to the mobile number linked to your Aadhaar
4. Enter the OTP and submit

*This works only if your mobile number is linked to Aadhaar.*

**Method 2 — Biometric (at CSC Centre)**
1. Visit the nearest CSC centre
2. Carry your Aadhaar card
3. The operator will do fingerprint/iris biometric verification
4. eKYC will be completed on the spot

If your mobile is not linked to Aadhaar, go to the CSC centre — biometric eKYC doesn't require a linked mobile.

## How to Update Bank Account or Mobile Number

Made a mistake in your bank details? Changed your mobile number? Here's how to fix it:

**To update bank account:**
1. Visit your nearest **PM Kisan nodal officer** at the Block or Tehsil level
2. Or raise a query through the PM Kisan helpline: **155261 / 1800115526** (toll-free)
3. Correction requests can also be submitted at the CSC centre

**To update Aadhaar-linked mobile number:**
Visit your nearest **Aadhaar enrolment centre** (post office or bank) to update the mobile number in your Aadhaar — that will automatically fix the OTP issue for eKYC.

## Land Seeding — Another Common Reason for Rejection

Many farmers registered correctly but their installments were stopped because of **land seeding failure** — meaning the land records in the scheme database don't match the state's land records (Bhulekh).

If your status shows **"Land Seeding Pending"**:
1. Visit your **Lekhpal / Patwari** (revenue officer) at the village or block level
2. They are authorised to seed your land records with the PM Kisan portal
3. Once seeded and verified, your pending installments will be released

This is a process that requires offline intervention — you can't fix it online yourself.

## Common Problems & Quick Fixes

| Problem | Likely Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| Installment not received | eKYC not done | Complete eKYC on pmkisan.gov.in |
| Name not in beneficiary list | Not registered | Do new registration at CSC or online |
| Payment sent but not in bank | Bank account details wrong | Contact nodal officer or helpline |
| Status shows "Rejected" | Land records mismatch | Visit Patwari / Lekhpal for land seeding |
| OTP not received for eKYC | Mobile not linked to Aadhaar | Go to CSC for biometric eKYC |
| Registered but no installment for 2+ years | Account may be frozen | Visit your bank branch and unfreeze it |

## Is ₹6,000/Year Enough?

Honestly? No — ₹6,000 a year barely covers seed costs for one crop season. But that's not really the point of PM Kisan. It was designed as a **supplementary income** to ease the cash flow pressure that farmers face between harvests, not to replace farm income entirely.

What matters is that it's **guaranteed, direct, and consistent**. For millions of small farmers in India, having ₹2,000 arrive in April, August, and December — without asking anyone for anything — has made a real difference.

If you qualify, make sure you're getting it.
