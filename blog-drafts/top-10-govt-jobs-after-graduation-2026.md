Four years of college are finally done. You have your degree in hand — and now comes the real question: *what next?*

If a stable, well-paying government job is on your mind, you're in the right place. Graduation opens doors to some of the most prestigious and high-paying sarkari naukri in India. We're talking about jobs that your relatives will talk about at family gatherings for years.

Here are the **top 10 government jobs you can target after graduation in 2026** — along with what they pay, how hard they are to crack, and what you need to do to get started.

---

## 1. UPSC Civil Services — IAS, IPS, IFS

Let's start with the big one. The **UPSC Civil Services Examination** is the most prestigious competitive exam in India — and it's open to any graduate, from any stream.

Clear it and you become an **IAS** (District Collector, Cabinet Secretary), **IPS** (Superintendent of Police, DGP), **IFS** (Indian Foreign Service), or any of the 24 other All India and Central Services.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Eligibility** | Graduation in any subject |
| **Age Limit** | 21–32 years (General); extra relaxation for OBC/SC/ST |
| **Salary** | ₹56,100 – ₹2,50,000/month depending on rank |
| **Attempts** | 6 (General), 9 (OBC), Unlimited (SC/ST) until age limit |
| **Stages** | Prelims → Mains → Interview |

Yes, it is hard. The success rate is around 0.1%. But the career it leads to is unmatched — power, respect, job security, and the ability to create real change. If you have the discipline and the drive, there is no better job in India.

**Where to start:** Read NCERT books Class 6–12 for basics, then move to standard references like Laxmikant (Polity), Bipin Chandra (History), and follow a newspaper daily.

---

## 2. SSC CGL — Staff Selection Commission Combined Graduate Level

SSC CGL is probably the **most popular government exam for graduates** in India. Lakhs of students appear every year, and for good reason — it offers a wide variety of central government jobs at a decent pay scale.

Posts you can get through SSC CGL:

- **Income Tax Inspector** (CBDT)
- **Central Excise Inspector** (CBIC)
- **Assistant Audit Officer** (CAG)
- **Sub-Inspector** (CBI, NIA)
- **Tax Assistant, Junior Statistical Officer**, and more

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Eligibility** | Any graduation |
| **Age Limit** | 18–32 years (post-dependent) |
| **Salary** | ₹25,500 – ₹1,51,100/month (Pay Level 4–8) |
| **Exam Stages** | Tier 1 (CBT) → Tier 2 (CBT) |
| **Conducting Body** | Staff Selection Commission |

SSC CGL is competitive but very much crackable with 6–8 months of focused preparation. The syllabus covers General Intelligence, English, Quantitative Aptitude, and General Awareness.

---

## 3. IBPS PO / SBI PO — Bank Probationary Officer

Banking is one of the most sought-after careers in India, and the **Probationary Officer (PO)** role is the fastest way in. You join as a trainee manager and within a few years, you're handling branch operations, lending, and customer services.

**Two main exams to target:**

- **IBPS PO** — recruits for public sector banks like PNB, Bank of Baroda, Canara Bank, Union Bank
- **SBI PO** — recruits for State Bank of India, the country's largest bank

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Eligibility** | Any graduation (60% marks for SBI) |
| **Age Limit** | 20–30 years |
| **Salary** | ₹41,960 – ₹63,840/month (plus allowances) |
| **Exam Stages** | Prelims → Mains → Interview |
| **Conducting Body** | IBPS / SBI |

Bank PO jobs offer **good salary, fast promotions, home loan benefits**, and postings across India. The work can be demanding at times, but the career growth is real — many bank POs become General Managers within 15–20 years.

---

## 4. State PSC — State Civil Services

Every state has its own Public Service Commission — **UPPSC, BPSC, MPPSC, TNPSC, KPSC** and so on. These exams recruit for state-level administrative, police, and revenue officers.

State civil services are often seen as slightly easier than UPSC but still require serious preparation. The advantage? **Postings in your home state**, and the competition pool is smaller.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Eligibility** | Any graduation |
| **Age Limit** | 21–40 years (varies by state) |
| **Salary** | ₹35,000 – ₹1,00,000/month (varies) |
| **Key Posts** | SDM, DSP, Block Development Officer, Tehsildar |
| **Exam Pattern** | Prelims → Mains → Interview (similar to UPSC) |

If your goal is to serve in administration but staying close to home matters to you, State PSC is the smarter choice over UPSC for many people.

## 5. RRB NTPC — Graduate Level Posts

We mentioned RRB NTPC for 12th pass candidates — but some posts are reserved specifically for **graduates** and come with higher pay and better roles:

- **Station Master**
- **Commercial Apprentice**
- **Traffic Assistant**
- **Goods Guard**
- **Senior Commercial cum Ticket Clerk**

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Eligibility** | Graduation (any stream) |
| **Age Limit** | 18–33 years |
| **Salary** | ₹29,200 – ₹74,500/month (Pay Level 5–6) |
| **Selection** | CBT Stage 1 → CBT Stage 2 → Skill Test / Document Verification |
| **Conducting Body** | Railway Recruitment Board |

Railway jobs remain one of the most stable careers in India. The perks — **free railway passes, medical benefits, subsidised housing** — are hard to match anywhere in the private sector.

## 6. CDS — Combined Defence Services (Army, Navy, Air Force Officer)

If you've ever dreamed of wearing officer's epaulettes, **CDS** is the exam that makes it happen. Conducted by UPSC twice a year, it recruits graduate men and women as commissioned officers in the three armed forces.

- **Indian Military Academy (IMA)** — Army Officer
- **Officers Training Academy (OTA)** — Short Service Commission
- **Indian Naval Academy** — Navy Officer
- **Air Force Academy** — Flying Branch / Ground Duty

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Eligibility** | Graduation (B.Sc for Air Force; any degree for Army/OTA) |
| **Age Limit** | 19–25 years (IMA/INA/AFA); up to 25 for OTA |
| **Salary** | ₹56,100/month starting + Military Service Pay + allowances |
| **Selection** | Written exam → SSB Interview (5-day process) |

The SSB interview is the most unique selection process you'll ever face — it tests your personality, leadership, and mental strength over 5 full days. Challenging, but incredibly character-building.

## 7. LIC AAO — Assistant Administrative Officer

LIC doesn't just hire Assistants (12th pass) — it also recruits **AAOs (Assistant Administrative Officers)** who are graduates. The AAO is a junior management role covering generalist, IT, legal, actuarial, and accounts functions.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Eligibility** | Graduation (stream varies by specialisation) |
| **Age Limit** | 21–30 years |
| **Salary** | ₹53,600 – ₹99,270/month (after training) |
| **Selection** | Prelims → Mains → Interview |

LIC AAO is one of the **best-paid insurance sector government jobs** in India. The interview component separates it from most other exams, so communication skills matter here.

## 8. Teaching — KVS, NVS & CTET Qualified Teacher

If you genuinely enjoy teaching, this path is both fulfilling and financially solid. After graduation (and a B.Ed in most cases), you can apply for:

- **KVS TGT / PGT** — Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan. Central government schools across India.
- **NVS Teacher** — Navodaya Vidyalaya Samiti. Residential schools for talented rural students.
- **CTET Qualified State Government Teacher** — State-run schools; recruitment through state boards.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Eligibility** | Graduation + B.Ed (for TGT/PGT); TGT = UG degree, PGT = PG degree |
| **Age Limit** | 18–35 years (KVS/NVS) |
| **Salary** | ₹35,400 – ₹1,12,400/month (TGT to PGT) |
| **Selection** | Written test + Interview / Demo lesson |

Teaching in a KV or NV school is genuinely prestigious. You get summers off, government housing in many postings, and the kind of job satisfaction that's rare in any field.

## 9. DRDO — Scientist B

For engineering and science graduates, **DRDO (Defence Research and Development Organisation)** recruits **Scientist B** every year through RAC (Recruitment and Assessment Centre). You'll work on defence technology, weapons systems, aerospace, electronics, and more.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Eligibility** | B.E / B.Tech or M.Sc in relevant disciplines |
| **Age Limit** | Up to 28 years (relaxation for reserved categories) |
| **Salary** | ₹56,100 – ₹1,77,500/month (Level 10) |
| **Selection** | Written test → Interview |

If you want to work on cutting-edge technology while serving the nation — and get a government pay package — DRDO is one of the best options available for science and engineering graduates.

---

## 10. CAG — Auditor & Accounts Officer

The **Comptroller and Auditor General of India (CAG)** recruits graduates for **Auditor and Junior Accounts Officer** posts through SSC CGL. It sounds less glamorous than some others on this list, but here's the reality: it's a prestigious central government job with excellent job security, decent pay, and very reasonable work hours.

You'll audit government departments, PSUs, and state finances — work that actually matters.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Eligibility** | Any graduation |
| **Age Limit** | 18–27 years |
| **Salary** | ₹29,200 – ₹92,300/month (Pay Level 5–8) |
| **Selection** | Through SSC CGL (Tier 1 + Tier 2) |

CAG postings are pan-India but the work culture is structured and professional. Many SSC CGL toppers specifically aim for CAG as their first preference.

## Which One is Right for You?

There's no single "best" government job after graduation — it depends on what you want from your career and life. Here's a quick filter:

- **Want power and impact?** → UPSC Civil Services or State PSC
- **Want the best salary for effort?** → SSC CGL or Bank PO
- **Want to serve the nation in uniform?** → CDS (Army / Navy / Air Force Officer)
- **Have a B.Tech / M.Sc?** → DRDO Scientist B is made for you
- **Want work-life balance?** → CAG Auditor or KVS Teacher
- **Want banking career growth?** → IBPS PO or SBI PO

One important thing people overlook — **don't just chase the most prestigious exam**. Chase the one you'll actually enjoy preparing for and working in. A motivated Bank PO will always be happier than a burnt-out IAS aspirant who gave 5 years and didn't clear.

Start with one target, go deep, and give it your best shot.

## How to Prepare Without Coaching

Most students assume they need expensive coaching to crack these exams. That's not true anymore. Here's what works in 2026:

1. **Free YouTube** — Unacademy, BYJU's, StudyIQ have free content for SSC, UPSC, and Banking
2. **Standard books** — NCERT + Laxmikant + R.S. Agarwal is enough for most exams
3. **Mock tests** — Testbook, PracticeMock, and Oliveboard offer free tests
4. **Newspaper habit** — 30 minutes of The Hindu or Indian Express daily changes your GK score

Consistency beats coaching every single time.
