## Answer draft (for "How to apply for Ladki Bahin Yojana in Maharashtra?")

You can apply for Mukhyamantri Majhi Ladki Bahin Yojana in three ways: the Nari Shakti Doot mobile app (fastest), the ladakibahin.maharashtra.gov.in web portal, or offline at any Anganwadi centre, Setu Suvidha Kendra, or Aaple Sarkar Seva Kendra. Rs. 1,500 lands in your bank account every month once approved, with roughly 30 working days between submission and first credit. Here is what to check before you start and how to move through the process cleanly.

**Step 1: Confirm you qualify before you touch the app.**

You need all five of these at the same time:

- Permanent resident of Maharashtra (Maharashtra domicile certificate, or a ration card showing a Maharashtra address for the last three years)
- Age 21 to 65 as of the application date. Unmarried women aged 21 to 25 are also eligible under the January 2025 amendment (they attach the father's income certificate instead of a spouse's)
- Family income below Rs. 2.5 lakh per year (fresh income certificate from the tehsildar, not older than 18 months)
- Aadhaar-seeded savings bank account with DBT active. Simply having the same name on Aadhaar and passbook is not enough. The bank has to actively map your account in the NPCI mapper. Check your seeding status on resident.uidai.gov.in before you apply.
- Neither you nor your spouse pays income tax, and neither is a government employee, MP, MLA, or owner of a four-wheeler other than a tractor

Only one woman per family qualifies. If your mother is already enrolled, you cannot register separately.

**Step 2: Keep these 8 documents ready.**

Aadhaar card, Maharashtra domicile certificate, ration card (yellow, orange, or white), Aadhaar-seeded bank passbook front page, income certificate under Rs. 2.5 lakh, marriage or widowhood or divorce certificate (whichever applies), recent passport photo, and the self-declaration form available on the portal or the app.

Two documents most people miss: the income certificate has to be current (not older than 18 months), and unmarried applicants aged 21 to 25 need the father's income certificate specifically labelled as such.

**Step 3: Apply through the Nari Shakti Doot mobile app (fastest path).**

Download Nari Shakti Doot from the Google Play Store. Check the publisher name is the Maharashtra Government Women and Child Development Department, because several fake lookalike apps have shown up since launch. The verified developer badge and 1 crore plus downloads confirm the official version.

Register with your Aadhaar-linked mobile number, complete OTP verification, and set a password. Choose "Apply for Scheme" and select Mukhyamantri Majhi Ladki Bahin Yojana from the dropdown. Enter your Aadhaar, ration card, and bank details. The portal runs a live Aadhaar seeding check and shows a green tick if your NPCI mapping is correct. Upload the 8 documents (each under 200 KB for PDFs, under 100 KB for JPGs) and submit.

You will get a 12-digit application reference number. Note it down. This is what you use to track status.

**Step 4: If the online route fails, use the offline fallback.**

Anganwadi centres are the closest for most rural applicants. There are 97,000 Anganwadis across Maharashtra and each is trained to accept the application on your behalf. The worker fills the form on a department-issued smartphone, scans your documents, and uploads for you. Setu Suvidha Kendras handle the same intake in taluk and district headquarters. Aaple Sarkar Seva Kendras cover urban clusters including Mumbai, Pune, and Nagpur.

Service centre charges are capped at Rs. 15 per form under the Maharashtra Right to Public Services Act. Any centre asking for more should be reported on helpline 181.

**Step 5: What happens next and how to check status.**

Your application moves through four verification stages: document check by the district cell (3 to 7 days), automated cross-check against income tax and GSTIN records (24 to 48 hours), field verification for a random 10 percent sample (adds 15 to 30 days if you are picked), and sanction plus payment mapping. The first Rs. 1,500 credit lands between the 15th and 25th of the month after sanction.

Payment status is tracked through the app dashboard (Beneficiary menu, Payment Status), the MHNSDDBT SMS from your bank, the pfms.nic.in portal (Know Your Payment section), or any Anganwadi worker who can print your statement for free.

**One thing many applicants got wrong in 2025:** the state ran a re-verification drive between January and April 2025 and removed around 5 lakh beneficiaries who exceeded the income cap, owned excluded assets, or fell into an excluded category. If you get a removal SMS from MHNSD, you have 60 days to appeal at the district Women and Child Development Officer with proof that disproves the removal reason.

**Comparison for context:** Ladki Bahin at Rs. 1,500 a month is not the highest state cash transfer for women. Karnataka's Gruha Lakshmi pays Rs. 2,000 a month but requires you to be head of family on the ration card. Odisha's Subhadra pays Rs. 10,000 a year in two lump-sum tranches plus a Subhadra RuPay card. Chhattisgarh's Mahtari Vandana pays Rs. 1,000 a month but restricts to married, widowed, or divorced women only. Maharashtra's advantage is the loosest eligibility screen, which is why the beneficiary base grew to 2.34 crore, the largest of any Indian state.

Detailed application guides and troubleshooting for the sibling schemes are here:

- [Gruha Lakshmi Yojana Karnataka: Rs. 2,000 a month at Seva Sindhu portal](https://www.sarkaridarapan.com/sarkari-yojana/gruha-lakshmi-yojana-karnataka)
- [Subhadra Yojana Odisha: Rs. 50,000 over 5 years plus RuPay card](https://www.sarkaridarapan.com/sarkari-yojana/subhadra-yojana-odisha)
- [Mahtari Vandana Yojana Chhattisgarh: Rs. 1,000 a month with June 2026 eKYC deadline](https://www.sarkaridarapan.com/sarkari-yojana/mahtari-vandana-yojana-chhattisgarh)

Verify Aadhaar seeding before you apply. That is the single biggest reason approved beneficiaries do not receive their first credit on time.

## Alternative answer draft (for "Which state gives the most money to women per month in India 2026?")

**Kept in case owner switches to the broader question.**

Karnataka's Gruha Lakshmi Yojana gives Rs. 2,000 every month to eligible women, which is the highest per-woman monthly cash transfer among all major Indian state schemes in 2026. But the ranking flips if you look at annual payout or eligibility ease. Here is how the top four state schemes actually compare.

**Rank by monthly amount:**

1. Karnataka Gruha Lakshmi: Rs. 2,000 per month. Rs. 24,000 a year. 1.28 crore women enrolled.
2. Maharashtra Ladki Bahin: Rs. 1,500 per month. Rs. 18,000 a year. 2.34 crore women enrolled (largest by count).
3. Chhattisgarh Mahtari Vandana: Rs. 1,000 per month. Rs. 12,000 a year. 70 lakh women enrolled.
4. Odisha Subhadra: pays Rs. 10,000 a year in two lump-sum tranches (Aug 15 and Mar 8), not monthly. Rs. 833 per month effective. 1.05 crore women enrolled.

**But monthly amount is not the only thing that matters.** Each scheme has a different eligibility screen, and some of the highest-paying schemes are the hardest to qualify for.

Karnataka's Gruha Lakshmi requires the woman to be recorded as head of family on the state ration card. If your husband or father-in-law is listed as head, you cannot apply directly. You have to first change the ration card head, which needs the current head's consent and 15 to 30 days at the Nada Kacheri office. In rural households, this social step can be harder than the paperwork.

Maharashtra's Ladki Bahin is the easiest to qualify for. Any woman aged 21 to 65 with household income under Rs. 2.5 lakh can register through the Nari Shakti Doot mobile app. No head-of-family filter. This is why Ladki Bahin has the largest beneficiary base at 2.34 crore, ahead of every other state scheme.

Chhattisgarh's Mahtari Vandana restricts to married, widowed, or divorced women only. Unmarried women do not qualify. There is also a mandatory eKYC recompletion by June 30, 2026 for existing beneficiaries. Miss the deadline and your monthly Rs. 1,000 stops from July 2026.

Odisha's Subhadra is the newest of the four (launched September 17, 2024) and is unique in two ways. First, it pays twice a year in Rs. 5,000 tranches instead of monthly. Second, every approved beneficiary gets a physical RuPay debit card in her name (issued by SBI, BOI, PNB, Canara, or UCO). The card can be used at ATMs and for card payments, which is a design choice aimed at pushing digital transaction adoption in rural Odisha.

**Which one you can actually claim depends on your state.** These are state schemes, not central schemes. A Karnataka woman cannot apply for Ladki Bahin. A Maharashtra woman cannot apply for Gruha Lakshmi. If you are in a state that does not have a similar scheme (Tamil Nadu, Kerala, West Bengal, and most north-east states currently have narrower alternatives), you still have five central government schemes that stack on top of anything the state offers:

1. PM Suraksha Bima Yojana (PMSBY): Rs. 2 lakh accident cover for Rs. 20 a year.
2. PM Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana (PMJJBY): Rs. 2 lakh life cover for Rs. 436 a year.
3. PM Matru Vandana Yojana (PMMVY): Rs. 5,000 for a first pregnancy, Rs. 6,000 for a second if the child is a girl.
4. Atal Pension Yojana (APY): Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 5,000 monthly pension from age 60 (contribution Rs. 42 to Rs. 291 based on entry age).
5. PM Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY): zero-balance bank account with Rs. 10,000 overdraft and Rs. 2 lakh accident cover.

**One thing that tripped many applicants up in 2025:** payments failed on schemes where the beneficiary's Aadhaar was not seeded correctly in the National Payments Corporation of India mapper. Simply having the same name on Aadhaar and passbook is not enough. The bank has to actively map your account. Verify seeding at resident.uidai.gov.in before applying to any of these schemes.

Detailed state-by-state guides on eligibility, application steps, and troubleshooting are available here:

- [Gruha Lakshmi Yojana Karnataka: full apply guide](https://www.sarkaridarapan.com/sarkari-yojana/gruha-lakshmi-yojana-karnataka)
- [Ladki Bahin Yojana Maharashtra: Nari Shakti Doot app walkthrough](https://www.sarkaridarapan.com/sarkari-yojana/majhi-ladki-bahin-yojana-maharashtra)
- [Subhadra Yojana Odisha: RuPay card and digital reward guide](https://www.sarkaridarapan.com/sarkari-yojana/subhadra-yojana-odisha)
- [Mahtari Vandana Yojana Chhattisgarh: eKYC deadline guide](https://www.sarkaridarapan.com/sarkari-yojana/mahtari-vandana-yojana-chhattisgarh)

Pick the scheme for your state, verify eligibility on the state portal, and complete the Aadhaar seeding step before you fill the application. The rest is straightforward.
