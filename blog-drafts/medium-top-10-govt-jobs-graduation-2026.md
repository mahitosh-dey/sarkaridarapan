Final year of college is done. You have your degree in hand, and now comes the real question: what next?

If a stable, well-paying government job is on your mind, you are in the right place. Graduation opens doors to some of the most prestigious and highest-paying sarkari naukri in India. These are the jobs your relatives will talk about at family gatherings for years.

Here are the top 10 government jobs to target after graduation in 2026, along with what they pay, how hard they are to crack, and what you need to do to get started.

## 1. UPSC Civil Services: IAS, IPS, IFS

The UPSC Civil Services Examination is the most prestigious competitive exam in India, and it is open to any graduate from any stream. Clear it and you become an IAS (District Collector, Cabinet Secretary), IPS (Superintendent of Police, DGP), IFS (Indian Foreign Service), or any of the 24 other All India and Central Services.

Eligibility is graduation in any subject. Age limit is 21 to 32 years for General category, with relaxation for OBC, SC, and ST. Salary ranges from Rs. 56,100 to Rs. 2,50,000 per month depending on rank. Attempts are capped at 6 for General, 9 for OBC, and unlimited for SC and ST until the age limit. Selection stages are Prelims, Mains, and Interview.

Yes, it is hard. The success rate is around 0.1%. But the career it leads to is unmatched: power, respect, job security, and the ability to create real change. If you have the discipline and drive, there is no better job in India.

To start, read NCERT books Class 6 to 12 for basics, then move to standard references like Laxmikant (Polity), Bipin Chandra (History), and follow a newspaper daily.

## 2. SSC CGL: Staff Selection Commission Combined Graduate Level

SSC CGL is probably the most popular government exam for graduates in India. Lakhs of students appear every year, and for good reason: it offers a wide variety of central government jobs at a decent pay scale.

Posts you can get through SSC CGL include Income Tax Inspector (CBDT), Central Excise Inspector (CBIC), Assistant Audit Officer (CAG), Sub-Inspector (CBI, NIA), Tax Assistant, and Junior Statistical Officer.

Eligibility is any graduation. Age limit ranges from 18 to 32 years depending on the post. Salary is Rs. 25,500 to Rs. 1,51,100 per month across Pay Level 4 to 8. The exam has two stages: Tier 1 (CBT) and Tier 2 (CBT). It is conducted by the Staff Selection Commission.

SSC CGL is competitive but very much crackable with 6 to 8 months of focused preparation. The syllabus covers General Intelligence, English, Quantitative Aptitude, and General Awareness.

## 3. IBPS PO and SBI PO: Bank Probationary Officer

Banking is one of the most sought-after careers in India, and the Probationary Officer role is the fastest way in. You join as a trainee manager and within a few years you are handling branch operations, lending, and customer service.

Two main exams to target: IBPS PO recruits for public sector banks like PNB, Bank of Baroda, Canara Bank, and Union Bank. SBI PO recruits for State Bank of India, the country's largest bank.

Eligibility is any graduation (60% marks required for SBI). Age limit is 20 to 30 years. Salary is Rs. 41,960 to Rs. 63,840 per month plus allowances. Selection is Prelims, Mains, and Interview. Conducting bodies are IBPS and SBI respectively.

Bank PO jobs offer good salary, fast promotions, home loan benefits, and postings across India. The work can be demanding, but the career growth is real. Many bank POs become General Managers within 15 to 20 years.

## 4. State PSC: State Civil Services

Every state has its own Public Service Commission: UPPSC, BPSC, MPPSC, TNPSC, KPSC, and so on. These exams recruit for state-level administrative, police, and revenue officers.

State civil services are often seen as slightly easier than UPSC but still require serious preparation. The advantage is postings in your home state, and the competition pool is smaller.

Eligibility is any graduation. Age limit varies by state, typically 21 to 40 years. Salary ranges from Rs. 35,000 to Rs. 1,00,000 per month depending on post and state. Key posts include SDM, DSP, Block Development Officer, and Tehsildar. Exam pattern is similar to UPSC with Prelims, Mains, and Interview.

If your goal is to serve in administration but staying close to home matters to you, State PSC is the smarter choice over UPSC for many people.

## 5. RRB NTPC: graduate level posts

Some RRB NTPC posts are reserved specifically for graduates and come with higher pay and better roles: Station Master, Commercial Apprentice, Traffic Assistant, Goods Guard, and Senior Commercial cum Ticket Clerk.

Eligibility is graduation in any stream. Age limit is 18 to 33 years. Salary is Rs. 29,200 to Rs. 74,500 per month across Pay Level 5 and 6. Selection involves CBT Stage 1, CBT Stage 2, and a skill test or document verification. The conducting body is the Railway Recruitment Board.

Railway jobs remain one of the most stable careers in India. The perks (free railway passes, medical benefits, subsidised housing) are hard to match anywhere in the private sector.

## 6. CDS: Combined Defence Services

If you have ever dreamed of wearing officer's epaulettes, CDS is the exam that makes it happen. Conducted by UPSC twice a year, it recruits graduate men and women as commissioned officers in the three armed forces.

Entry points include Indian Military Academy (IMA) for Army Officer, Officers Training Academy (OTA) for Short Service Commission, Indian Naval Academy for Navy Officer, and Air Force Academy for Flying Branch or Ground Duty.

Eligibility is graduation, with B.Sc required for Air Force and any degree acceptable for Army or OTA. Age limit is 19 to 25 years for IMA, INA, and AFA, and up to 25 for OTA. Salary starts at Rs. 56,100 per month plus Military Service Pay and allowances. Selection is a written exam followed by SSB Interview over 5 full days.

The SSB interview is the most unique selection process you will ever face. It tests your personality, leadership, and mental strength. Challenging, but incredibly character-building.

## 7. LIC AAO: Assistant Administrative Officer

LIC recruits AAOs (Assistant Administrative Officers) who are graduates. The AAO is a junior management role covering generalist, IT, legal, actuarial, and accounts functions.

Eligibility is graduation, with stream varying by specialisation. Age limit is 21 to 30 years. Salary after training is Rs. 53,600 to Rs. 99,270 per month. Selection is Prelims, Mains, and Interview.

LIC AAO is one of the best-paid insurance sector government jobs in India. The interview component separates it from most other exams, so communication skills matter here.

## 8. Teaching: KVS, NVS, and CTET qualified teacher

If you genuinely enjoy teaching, this path is both fulfilling and financially solid. After graduation (and a B.Ed in most cases), you can apply for KVS TGT/PGT (Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan, central government schools across India), NVS Teacher (Navodaya Vidyalaya Samiti, residential schools for talented rural students), and CTET qualified State Government Teacher (state-run schools, recruitment through state boards).

Eligibility requires graduation plus B.Ed for TGT and PGT posts, where TGT needs a UG degree and PGT needs a PG degree. Age limit is 18 to 35 years for KVS and NVS. Salary ranges from Rs. 35,400 for TGT to Rs. 1,12,400 for PGT per month. Selection is a written test followed by interview or a demo lesson.

Teaching in a KV or NV school is genuinely prestigious. You get summers off, government housing in many postings, and the kind of job satisfaction that is rare in any field.

## 9. DRDO: Scientist B

For engineering and science graduates, DRDO (Defence Research and Development Organisation) recruits Scientist B every year through the Recruitment and Assessment Centre. You will work on defence technology, weapons systems, aerospace, and electronics.

Eligibility is B.E, B.Tech, or M.Sc in relevant disciplines. Age limit is up to 28 years with relaxation for reserved categories. Salary is Rs. 56,100 to Rs. 1,77,500 per month at Level 10. Selection is a written test followed by an interview.

If you want to work on cutting-edge technology while serving the nation and get a government pay package, DRDO is one of the best options available for science and engineering graduates.

## 10. CAG: Auditor and Accounts Officer

The Comptroller and Auditor General of India (CAG) recruits graduates for Auditor and Junior Accounts Officer posts through SSC CGL. It sounds less glamorous than some others on this list, but it is a prestigious central government job with excellent security, decent pay, and reasonable work hours. You will audit government departments, PSUs, and state finances: work that matters.

Eligibility is any graduation. Age limit is 18 to 27 years. Salary is Rs. 29,200 to Rs. 92,300 per month across Pay Level 5 to 8. Selection is through SSC CGL, Tier 1 and Tier 2.

CAG postings are pan-India but the work culture is structured and professional. Many SSC CGL toppers specifically aim for CAG as their first preference.

## Quick summary

Here is a one-line take on each option so you can compare at a glance.

**UPSC Civil Services** pays Rs. 56,100 to Rs. 2,50,000 per month and is very hard to clear. Best for ambitious all-rounders with a 3 to 5 year prep window.

**SSC CGL** pays Rs. 25,500 to Rs. 1,51,100 per month and is medium-to-hard. Best for any graduate with 6 to 8 months of focused prep.

**IBPS PO or SBI PO** pays Rs. 41,960 to Rs. 63,840 per month and is medium difficulty. Best for finance-minded candidates wanting fast promotion.

**State PSC** pays Rs. 35,000 to Rs. 1,00,000 per month and is hard. Best for candidates wanting home state postings.

**RRB NTPC Graduate** pays Rs. 29,200 to Rs. 74,500 per month and is medium difficulty. Best for stability seekers who value railway perks.

**CDS** pays Rs. 56,100 and above per month and is hard. Best for defence aspirants who can handle the 5-day SSB interview.

**LIC AAO** pays Rs. 53,600 to Rs. 99,270 per month and is medium difficulty. Best for insurance and finance-inclined graduates.

**KVS or NVS Teacher** pays Rs. 35,400 to Rs. 1,12,400 per month and is medium difficulty. Best for those genuinely passionate about teaching.

**DRDO Scientist B** pays Rs. 56,100 to Rs. 1,77,500 per month and is hard. Best for engineering and science graduates.

**CAG Auditor** pays Rs. 29,200 to Rs. 92,300 per month and is medium difficulty. Best for commerce graduates wanting structured work culture.

## Which one is right for you?

There is no single best government job after graduation. It depends on what you want from your career and life. Here is a quick filter.

If you want power and impact, aim for UPSC Civil Services or State PSC. If you want the best salary for effort, SSC CGL or Bank PO. If you want to serve the nation in uniform, CDS. If you have a B.Tech or M.Sc, DRDO Scientist B is a natural fit. If you want work-life balance, CAG Auditor or KVS Teacher. If you want banking career growth, IBPS PO or SBI PO.

One thing people often overlook: do not just chase the most prestigious exam. Chase the one you will enjoy preparing for and working in. A motivated Bank PO will always be happier than a burnt-out IAS aspirant who gave 5 years and did not clear.

Start with one target, go deep, and give it your best shot.

## How to prepare without coaching

Most students assume they need expensive coaching to crack these exams. That is not true anymore. What works in 2026:

Free YouTube channels like Unacademy, BYJU's, and StudyIQ have free content for SSC, UPSC, and Banking. Standard books like NCERT plus Laxmikant plus R.S. Aggarwal are enough for most exams. Mock tests on Testbook, PracticeMock, and Oliveboard offer free tests. A newspaper habit of 30 minutes of The Hindu or Indian Express daily changes your GK score.

Consistency beats coaching every single time.

## Staying updated on notifications

Government job calendars are unpredictable. Notifications drop without much warning and application windows close in 2 to 3 weeks. Bookmark a reliable sarkari naukri portal (SarkariDarapan updates central and state vacancies daily). Set Google Alerts for your target exam name. Follow official SSC, UPSC, IBPS, and state PSC websites directly. Join subject-specific Telegram preparation groups.

Missing the application window after months of preparation is heartbreaking. Do not let it happen.
