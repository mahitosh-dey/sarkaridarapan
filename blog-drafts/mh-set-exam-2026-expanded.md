## MH SET Exam 2026 registration, exam date, and how to apply

The Maharashtra State Eligibility Test 2026 (MH SET Exam 2026) closes for applications on June 16, 2026, with the exam scheduled for July 26, 2026. Savitribai Phule Pune University (SPPU) conducts the test on behalf of the Government of Maharashtra. Anyone planning to become an Assistant Professor in a college affiliated to a Maharashtra or Goa university needs this certificate before recruitment.

## How to apply for MH SET Exam 2026 step by step

Applications go through setexam.unipune.ac.in only. The board does not accept offline forms.

1. Visit setexam.unipune.ac.in and click "MH SET 2026 Registration".
2. Enter your name, date of birth, category, and a valid email plus mobile number. You will receive a registration ID.
3. Log in with the registration ID and fill in academic details, subject choice, and preferred exam city.
4. Upload a passport photo (max 100 KB, JPG) and signature (max 40 KB, JPG).
5. Pay the fee online via net banking, debit card, or UPI.
6. Download the confirmation page and print two copies. Keep one for the exam day.

The correction window on June 18 to 20 lets you fix any error in name, subject, or category. After that, no change is allowed.

## Eligibility for MH SET Exam 2026

You are eligible if you have a Master's degree in the subject you want to appear in, with at least 55% marks (50% for OBC, SC, ST, PwD, and third-gender candidates). Final-year Master's students can apply provisionally but must submit their mark sheet within two years of clearing the exam.

There is no upper age limit.

### Three quick worked examples

- Priya, MA English, 58%, general category: eligible. She can appear in the English subject paper.
- Rahul, MSc Physics, 53%, OBC: eligible. His 53% clears the 50% OBC threshold.
- Anjali, MCom, 49%, SC: not eligible for the 2026 attempt. She needs 50% minimum. She could upgrade her marks through a bridge program or re-attempt select papers if her university allows.

PhD holders who completed their doctorate under UGC 2009 or later regulations are exempt from SET/NET for direct Assistant Professor recruitment, though the SET certificate still helps in interviews.

## MH SET Exam 2026 pattern and marking

MH SET has two papers on the same day, held in a single sitting with no break.

**Paper I: Teaching and research aptitude**
Compulsory for every candidate. 50 questions, 100 marks, 60 minutes. Sections cover teaching aptitude, research aptitude, reading comprehension, communication, reasoning (including mathematical reasoning), data interpretation, ICT, people and environment, and higher education system.

**Paper II: Subject-specific**
100 questions, 200 marks, 120 minutes. You appear in the subject of your post-graduation.

No negative marking in either paper. Every correct answer gets 2 marks.

## Paper I topic-by-topic breakdown

Paper I is where most candidates lose or gain their edge because it is the same for every subject and rewards consistent scoring. Here is what each of the 9 topic areas tests:

- **Teaching aptitude:** teaching methods, learner characteristics, factors affecting teaching, teaching support systems. About 5 to 7 questions.
- **Research aptitude:** research methods, thesis writing, research ethics, IPR basics. 4 to 6 questions.
- **Reading comprehension:** two passages of about 500 words each, 5 questions per passage. Practice with previous year passages.
- **Communication:** types of communication, barriers, mass media, and effective communication. 4 to 5 questions.
- **Reasoning including mathematical:** number series, letter series, coding-decoding, and basic mathematical reasoning. 6 to 8 questions.
- **Logical reasoning:** venn diagrams, syllogisms, analogies. 4 to 5 questions.
- **Data interpretation:** table-based, bar chart, pie chart interpretation. 4 to 5 questions.
- **ICT:** internet basics, e-learning, MS Office fundamentals, computer terminology. 3 to 5 questions.
- **People, development, and environment:** current environmental issues, sustainable development, environmental policies. 3 to 4 questions.
- **Higher education system:** governance of higher education in India, UGC, AICTE, professional councils, institutions of higher learning. 3 to 5 questions.

Scoring 65 marks out of 100 on Paper I is a realistic and often decisive target.

## Subjects offered in MH SET Exam 2026

Over 60 subjects are available. The most popular are:

- Humanities: History, Political Science, Sociology, Public Administration, Marathi, Hindi, English, Sanskrit
- Commerce and Management: Commerce, Business Administration, Home Science
- Sciences: Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, Botany, Zoology, Environmental Science, Geography, Earth Science
- Professional: Computer Science, Electronic Science, Library and Information Science, Physical Education, Law, Journalism and Mass Communication

You must appear in the subject that matches your post-graduation. A candidate with MA Political Science cannot appear in the Economics paper.

## MH SET 2026 exam centres

The exam is held at 30+ centres across Maharashtra and 2 in Goa. Popular cities include Mumbai, Pune, Nagpur, Nashik, Aurangabad, Kolhapur, Solapur, Amravati, Thane, and Panaji (Goa).

At registration, you select up to three preferred cities in order of priority. The final centre allotment is confirmed on your admit card and is generally the first or second preference. Once allotted, the centre cannot be changed. Plan travel and accommodation accordingly, especially if you are allotted a centre 2 to 3 hours from your home city.

Reporting is at the exam centre, not the university campus, so read your admit card carefully for the exact address.

## Exam day guide for MH SET 2026

The exam runs in a single sitting from 10:00 AM to 1:00 PM (Paper I from 10:00 to 11:00, Paper II from 11:00 to 1:00, no break).

- **Reach by 8:30 AM.** Gates typically close at 9:30 AM. Late entry is not allowed.
- **Carry these to the centre:** printed admit card (2 copies), a valid original photo ID (Aadhaar, PAN, Driving Licence, Passport, or Voter ID), and 2 recent passport photos matching the one uploaded.
- **What to bring for writing:** blue or black ballpoint pen only. Pencils, correction fluid, and gel pens are not allowed for OMR marking.
- **What is banned inside the hall:** mobile phones, smartwatches, Bluetooth devices, calculators, wallets, bags, printed material, and any electronic gadget. Storage outside the hall is at your own risk, so travel light.
- **Dress code:** no formal dress code, but comfortable full-sleeve clothing is recommended because centres often keep AC low. Avoid heavy metallic jewellery to speed up frisking.
- **After the exam:** you keep your admit card, not the OMR. Store your admit card safely because you will need it for the answer key objection window and any downstream correspondence.

## Reservation and category-wise cutoff mechanics

MH SET's qualifying formula has two stages, both of which must clear:

1. **Aggregate cutoff across both papers combined**: 40 percent for General, EWS, and Open category candidates; 35 percent for OBC, SC, ST, PwD, and Third-gender candidates.
2. **Top 6 percent per subject, per category**: from the pool of candidates who cleared step 1, the top 6 percent in each subject and each category are declared SET-passed.

The second stage is the actual bottleneck. A candidate scoring 45 percent may still not qualify if their subject and category pool is heavily contested, while a candidate scoring 41 percent could qualify in a less-competitive subject or category. Cutoffs are not fixed in absolute marks and are calculated post-result.

## Preparation strategy for MH SET Exam 2026

You have roughly 45 days between the last application date and the exam. That is enough time if you already know your subject well.

- Weeks 1 and 2: solve 5 years of previous year papers for Paper I. This alone tells you which topics MH SET repeats.
- Weeks 3 and 4: cover your Paper II syllabus from your PG textbooks. Do not buy a new "MH SET guide". The standard university textbooks are enough.
- Weeks 5 and 6: full-length mocks, one every alternate day. Aim for 65 percent in Paper I and 55 percent in Paper II.

Candidates who cleared past cycles most often recommend KVS Madaan (Pearson) and Rukmini Bhaya Nair for Paper I, along with the free NTA UGC-NET Paper I resource on the official portal.

Most serious aspirants attempt both. Clearing either one qualifies you for teaching in Maharashtra colleges. Clearing UGC-NET gives you national mobility.

## What happens after you clear MH SET

The MH SET certificate has lifetime validity. Once you clear, you can:

1. Apply to any Maharashtra or Goa college recruiting Assistant Professors (watch openings on collegerecruitment.mahaonline.gov.in and individual college websites).
2. Appear for university NET-cleared teaching positions.
3. Register on the government's SET-passed candidate portal for direct interview calls.

The certificate does not guarantee a job. It is a minimum eligibility filter. Actual recruitment depends on individual college interviews, which weigh subject knowledge, teaching demo, and (increasingly) research output.

## The reality of Assistant Professor recruitment after MH SET

Passing the exam is step one. Actual recruitment in Maharashtra runs on three tracks:

- **Government (aided) colleges:** highest job security and best pay (7th CPC, Rs. 57,700 to Rs. 1,82,400 entry level). Recruitment happens through college-wise notifications, but appointment is usually approved by the state Department of Higher Education. Interview panels are strict; PhD, publications, and NET/SET together are often expected.
- **Unaided private colleges:** salaries range Rs. 25,000 to Rs. 50,000 per month at entry level, sometimes lower. Fewer perks but faster hiring. Many candidates use these as a first step before moving to aided colleges.
- **Autonomous and deemed universities:** apply directly via their own recruitment portals. Selection processes vary widely and often skip the state approval step.

Publication record and PhD status matter more than raw MH SET scores in interviews. Start planning your first UGC-CARE listed paper as soon as you clear.

## Common mistakes to avoid

- Applying in the wrong subject. The subject you appear in must match your PG degree exactly. A candidate with MA Sociology cannot appear in the Political Science paper even if the syllabi overlap.
- Uploading oversized documents. The portal silently rejects photos above 100 KB. Compress before uploading.
- Missing the correction window. After June 20, no change is allowed. Verify every field before June 17 payment.
- Waiting for the "final" answer key to challenge questions. Objections must be raised during the provisional answer key window, not after.
- Skipping the mock test phase because "I know the syllabus". Time management on OMR is a distinct skill; without practice, most candidates finish Paper II 10 to 15 minutes late.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is MH SET Exam 2026 online or offline?**
MH SET Exam 2026 is a pen-and-paper (OMR) exam held at physical centres across Maharashtra and Goa on July 26, 2026. It is not a computer-based test.

**How many attempts are allowed for MH SET?**
There is no cap on the number of attempts. You can appear every year until you clear.

**What is the passing percentage for MH SET Exam 2026?**
Candidates need at least 40 percent aggregate across both papers for General category and 35 percent for reserved categories. From this qualifying pool, the top 6 percent per subject per category are declared SET-passed.

**Can final-year PG students apply for MH SET Exam 2026?**
Yes. Final-year Master's candidates can apply provisionally but must submit their mark sheet within two years of the result date.

**Is MH SET valid outside Maharashtra?**
No. MH SET is valid only in Maharashtra and Goa. For other states, either clear that state's SET (Karnataka SET, Kerala SET, and similar) or clear UGC-NET which is valid nationally.

**How do I download the MH SET 2026 admit card?**
Admit cards release in the second week of July on setexam.unipune.ac.in. Log in with your registration ID and password. Download and print two copies.

**Is there negative marking in MH SET Exam 2026?**
No. There is no negative marking in either Paper I or Paper II. Every correct answer gets 2 marks, and wrong or unanswered questions get zero. Attempt every question even if you are unsure.

**Can I change my exam centre after applying?**
No. Once your admit card is issued, the centre is fixed. Plan travel to your allotted centre in advance. This is why choosing three realistic preferred cities during registration matters.

**How is the MH SET result calculated?**
The result is based on your combined score across both papers, computed as a percentage of total marks (300). You must clear the category aggregate threshold and then rank in the top 6 percent per subject per category.

**When will the MH SET 2026 certificate be issued?**
The digital certificate is available for download on setexam.unipune.ac.in within 4 to 8 weeks of the result declaration. It carries lifetime validity for Assistant Professor recruitment in Maharashtra and Goa.
