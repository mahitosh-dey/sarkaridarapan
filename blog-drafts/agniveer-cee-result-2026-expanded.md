## How to check Agniveer Army CEE Result 2026

The Agniveer Army CEE (Common Entrance Examination) Result 2026 is published on joinindianarmy.nic.in. Candidates who qualified the Physical Fitness Test and Medical Examination at their regional rally and then sat the online CEE can check their qualifying status using the Roll Number printed on their admit card and their date of birth. This guide covers the exact steps to check the result, how to read the result slip, how the merit list is built, and what happens next for both selected and non-selected candidates.

## Step-by-step: check your CEE result

1. Open the official portal at [joinindianarmy.nic.in](https://joinindianarmy.nic.in).
2. Look for the banner or link titled "Agniveer CEE Result 2026" on the homepage, or navigate to the Recruitment section.
3. Click the result link for your recruitment batch (batch identifier is on your admit card).
4. Enter your Roll Number and Date of Birth (DD/MM/YYYY).
5. Submit and view your result status on screen.
6. Download the PDF result slip and save two copies (one soft, one printout).

Keep your admit card and PFT (Physical Fitness Test) card handy while checking. If the portal is slow near result release, try again after 30 minutes rather than refreshing repeatedly, which sometimes locks the session.

## Understanding your CEE result slip

Your result slip is a single-page PDF that carries the following fields:

- Roll Number and Recruitment Batch ID
- Full Name (as per your Class 10 marksheet)
- Category applied for: GD, Technical, Clerk/SKT, Tradesman (Class 10), or Tradesman (Class 8)
- Marks obtained in the CEE
- Qualifying status: "Qualified", "Not Qualified", or occasionally "Result Awaited"
- Reporting instructions (only if qualified)

The qualifying status alone doesn't guarantee a joining letter. Final selection depends on where your marks place you in the regional merit list for your category. A "Qualified" status means you have cleared the minimum threshold; a joining offer depends on the vacancy count for your Army Recruiting Office (ARO) region and category.

## Recruitment categories covered

Agniveer Army recruitment runs across five main categories. Each has its own eligibility, its own exam structure, and its own regional cutoff.

| Category | Qualification required | Exam duration |
|---|---|---|
| Soldier General Duty (GD) | Class 10, 45% aggregate | 60 minutes |
| Soldier Technical | Class 12 with PCM and English, 50% aggregate | 60 minutes |
| Soldier Clerk / Store Keeper Technical (SKT) | Class 12 (any stream), 60% aggregate, 50% in English and Math | 60 minutes |
| Soldier Tradesman (Class 10) | Class 10, 33% in each subject | 60 minutes |
| Soldier Tradesman (Class 8) | Class 8 pass (for Cook, House Keeper, Mess Keeper, Gardener, Safaiwala) | 60 minutes |

Your result is category-specific. Applying under Technical does not make you eligible for GD merit ranking, even if your marks would qualify under GD.

## How the CEE is scored and how the merit list is built

The CEE is an online, multiple-choice test with a 60-minute time limit. The total marks and section structure vary by category. GD and Technical papers carry 100 marks (50 questions of 2 marks each); the Clerk/SKT paper carries 200 marks across four sections (General Knowledge, General Science, Math, and English). Tradesman-10 carries 100 marks and Tradesman-8 carries 50 marks. Refer to your admit card for the exact split for your paper.

Negative marking applies. Per the Indian Army recruitment notification, wrong answers deduct a proportion of the marks awarded for a correct answer, so blind guessing costs you more than skipping a question you don't know.

The final merit list is not built on CEE marks alone. It is a composite of:

- Marks from the Physical Fitness Test (1.6 km run, pull-ups, 9-feet ditch, zig-zag balance) conducted at the rally
- Marks from the Physical Measurement Test (height, chest, weight)
- Marks obtained in the CEE

The CEE is the largest contributor for GD and Technical, but PFT marks decide the rank order among candidates who tie on CEE. This is why a candidate with slightly lower CEE marks but a top-tier 1.6 km time can outrank one with a higher CEE score but a slower run.

Regional cutoffs are not published centrally. Each ARO releases its own zone-wise merit list based on the number of vacancies notified for that region and the pool of qualified candidates. A qualifying score in one ARO may not translate to a joining letter in another.

### What "Qualified", "Not Qualified" and "Result Awaited" mean

**Qualified**: You have cleared the CEE minimum threshold for your category and are on the regional merit list. A joining letter will follow if your rank falls within the vacancy count for your ARO.

**Not Qualified**: You did not clear the minimum threshold, or your composite score (PFT plus PMT plus CEE) fell below the regional cutoff for your ARO.

**Result Awaited**: Usually a temporary state during answer-key objection windows or when a candidate's PFT or medical documents are still under review. Check again after 48 hours.

## After the result: document verification

Selected candidates receive reporting orders that specify the date and location for document verification (DV). Original documents are checked physically. Missing or mismatched documents lead to disqualification at this stage, even if your CEE rank is good.

Bring originals of the following:

- Class 10 and Class 12 marksheets and passing certificates
- School Leaving Certificate or Transfer Certificate
- Caste certificate (OBC, SC, ST) if applicable, on the prescribed central government format
- Domicile certificate matching the state you applied from
- Character certificate from a gazetted officer or village Sarpanch, dated within the last 6 months
- Recent passport-size colour photographs (usually 20 copies)
- Aadhaar card and PAN card
- Marital status certificate (unmarried certificate) for candidates below 21

Common reasons for rejection at DV: name mismatch across documents, invalid caste certificate format, expired character certificate, or a domicile certificate from a state different from the ARO region.

## Training and the Seva Nidhi package

Candidates who clear DV and final medical are enrolled and sent to the regimental training centre for their arm or service. Training typically lasts 24 to 31 weeks depending on the regiment. Agniveer GD candidates go to the Regimental Training Centres of their respective Infantry regiments; Technical candidates go to arm-specific centres.

Agniveers serve for 4 years. The monthly package, per the Ministry of Defence Agniveer scheme notification of June 2022, is as follows:

| Year | Monthly package | In-hand pay | Contribution to Seva Nidhi (per person) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Year 1 | Rs. 30,000 | Rs. 21,000 | Rs. 9,000 |
| Year 2 | Rs. 33,000 | Rs. 23,100 | Rs. 9,900 |
| Year 3 | Rs. 36,500 | Rs. 25,580 | Rs. 10,950 |
| Year 4 | Rs. 40,000 | Rs. 28,000 | Rs. 12,000 |

The government matches each Agniveer's Seva Nidhi contribution rupee for rupee. The total Seva Nidhi corpus at the end of the 4-year term is approximately Rs. 11.71 lakh, paid tax-free per the scheme notification. 25% of each batch is offered continued regular Army service after the 4-year term, selection based on merit and organisational requirement.

## What to do if you did not qualify

Not clearing the CEE this cycle does not close the door. You can reapply in the next Agniveer recruitment cycle provided you still fall within the 17.5 to 21 age bracket at the time of that new rally. In parallel, other central and state recruitments run on similar timelines and eligibility.

| Alternate path | Eligibility overlap with Agniveer Army | Typical notification window |
|---|---|---|
| Agniveer Navy (SSR, MR) | Class 10 or 12, age 17.5 to 21 | January and July |
| Agniveer Air Force (Vayu) | Class 10 or 12 depending on stream, age 17.5 to 21 | January and June |
| SSC GD Constable (CAPF, NIA, SSF, Assam Rifles) | Class 10, age 18 to 23 | September to November |
| State Police Constable (varies by state) | Class 10 or 12, age varies (usually 18 to 25) | State-notified, rolling |
| CAPF Constable Tradesman (BSF, CRPF, ITBP, CISF, SSB) | Class 10, age 18 to 23 | Yearly, usually first half |

Prepare by studying the areas where you lost marks in the CEE (usually General Knowledge or Mathematics for GD candidates) and by keeping your PFT timing tight. Most alternate paths share the same physical fitness expectations.

## Common portal errors and fixes

- **"Invalid Roll Number"**: Check that you are entering the exact Roll Number from your Admit Card, not your Registration Number or Application ID. These are different numbers.
- **Result link not visible**: The result is released ARO-by-ARO, not centrally. Your ARO's result may not be released on the same day as another ARO's. Check the "Result Notice" section for release dates by region.
- **Portal times out during peak hours**: Traffic spikes in the first hour of release. Try again after 30 to 60 minutes.
- **PDF download fails**: Right-click the download link, choose "Save link as", and save to your device. Some browsers block direct PDF opens.
- **DOB format rejected**: The portal accepts DD/MM/YYYY. Do not use hyphens or type the year first.
- **Result shows "Result Awaited" for several days**: This usually means your PFT or Medical papers are under review. Contact your ARO office directly if the status persists beyond 10 days.

## Frequently asked questions

**How do I check my Agniveer Army CEE Result 2026?**
Go to joinindianarmy.nic.in, click on the result link for your recruitment batch, enter your Roll Number and Date of Birth, and download your PDF result slip. Save two copies.

**What documents do I need to check the result online?**
You need your Roll Number (from your Admit Card) and your Date of Birth. No other documents are needed to view the result. Documents are required only at the physical verification stage after selection.

**My result shows "Not Qualified". Can I reapply?**
Yes. Candidates who don't qualify in one Agniveer Army recruitment cycle can apply in the next cycle, provided they still fall within the 17.5 to 21 age bracket at the time of that rally. Your PFT and medical results from the earlier attempt do not carry over.

**What is the Seva Nidhi amount for Agniveer Army 2026?**
The total Seva Nidhi corpus at the end of 4 years of Agniveer service is approximately Rs. 11.71 lakh, tax-free. It is built from equal contributions by the Agniveer and the government, per the Ministry of Defence Agniveer scheme notification of June 2022.

**Is 25% permanent enrolment confirmed for every batch?**
Yes. The Agniveer scheme specifies that 25% of each batch will be offered the opportunity to continue in regular Army service after 4 years, based on merit and organisational requirement, per the June 2022 scheme announcement.

**Where is the CEE conducted?**
The CEE is conducted online at designated exam centres across India. Your Admit Card, issued about 10 days before the exam, specifies your exam centre, date, and reporting time. The CEE is not conducted at the rally ground.

**How is the merit list built for Agniveer Army selection?**
The final merit list is a composite of your CEE marks, your Physical Fitness Test marks (1.6 km run, pull-ups, 9-feet ditch, zig-zag balance), and your Physical Measurement Test marks (height, chest, weight). Cutoffs are set region-by-region by each Army Recruiting Office based on notified vacancies and the qualified candidate pool.

**Is there negative marking in the Agniveer Army CEE?**
Yes. Per the recruitment notification, wrong answers deduct a proportion of the marks awarded for a correct answer. Blind guessing costs more than skipping a question you don't know.

**Can I check the result offline through my ARO office?**
Yes. If you can't access the portal, visit your Army Recruiting Office in person with your Admit Card. Results are also posted on ARO notice boards. The online portal is the fastest option for candidates outside the ARO city.

**When will the joining letter arrive after "Qualified" status?**
Joining letters typically arrive 6 to 10 weeks after the result, following document verification and final medical clearance. The letter is sent by registered post to the address on your application form.
