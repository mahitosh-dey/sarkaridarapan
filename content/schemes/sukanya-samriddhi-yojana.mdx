## Why SSY Beats Almost Every Other Savings Option for Your Daughter

When it comes to putting money aside for a daughter's future, Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana is hard to beat. At 8.2% interest per annum with full tax exemption at every stage, no bank FD or mutual fund SIP comes with this combination of guaranteed returns and tax efficiency. The government clearly wants parents to invest in their girls, and the interest rate reflects that intent.

Launched on January 22, 2015, as part of the Beti Bachao Beti Padhao campaign, SSY has crossed 3 crore accounts. The scheme is simple in design -- you deposit money regularly for 15 years, the account matures when your daughter turns 21, and the accumulated corpus with compound interest is paid out. Along the way, you save tax on every deposit, the interest is tax-free, and the maturity amount is tax-free. This EEE (Exempt-Exempt-Exempt) status is something only PPF shares among government schemes, and SSY offers a significantly higher interest rate.

## SSY vs PPF -- A Quick Comparison

People often ask whether they should go for SSY or PPF. Here is a straightforward look:

| Feature | SSY | PPF | FD (5 Year) | NSC |
|---------|-----|-----|-------------|-----|
| Interest Rate | 8.2% | 7.1% | 6-7% | 7.7% |
| Tax on Deposit | 80C Exempt | 80C Exempt | 80C Exempt | 80C Exempt |
| Tax on Interest | Exempt | Exempt | Taxable | Taxable |
| Tax on Maturity | Exempt | Exempt | Taxable | Taxable |
| Lock-in Period | 21 years | 15 years | 5 years | 5 years |
| Min Deposit/Year | Rs. 250 | Rs. 500 | Rs. 1,000 | Rs. 1,000 |
| Max Deposit/Year | Rs. 1.5 lakh | Rs. 1.5 lakh | No limit | No limit |

The 1.1% difference between SSY and PPF might not sound like much, but over 21 years of compounding, it adds up to lakhs of rupees. If you have a daughter under 10 and the question is where to park long-term savings for her, SSY wins hands down.

## Where to Open the Account

The post office is where most SSY accounts are opened. There is a branch in nearly every town and large village, the staff is familiar with the scheme, and the process is straightforward. Walk in, ask for the Sukanya Samriddhi account opening form, fill it out, attach the documents, make your first deposit, and you get a passbook.

Banks also offer SSY accounts -- SBI, PNB, Bank of Baroda, Bank of India, Canara Bank, ICICI, Axis, HDFC, and several others have it. The advantage of opening at a bank is that you might be able to link it to internet banking for online deposits, which saves you the trip to the branch every time you want to put money in.

You will need the girl child's birth certificate, your own ID and address proof, and a couple of passport-sized photos. The whole process takes about 30-45 minutes.

## The Rs. 250 Rule -- Do Not Ignore This

The minimum annual deposit is just Rs. 250. Even families with very modest incomes can keep the account running. But here is what catches people: if you fail to deposit even Rs. 250 in a financial year, the account gets classified as "defaulted."

Revival costs Rs. 50 penalty per year of default, plus you have to pay the missed minimum deposits and the current year's deposit. It is not the end of the world, but it is an unnecessary hassle. Set a reminder for February or March every year -- even if you deposit only Rs. 250, the account stays active.

The maximum you can put in is Rs. 1.5 lakh per year, which also happens to be the Section 80C limit. So if SSY is your primary tax-saving instrument, you can max out the 80C deduction with this single account.

## The Tax Benefit Is Genuinely Powerful

SSY enjoys triple exemption:
1. **Deposits** qualify for deduction under Section 80C (up to Rs. 1.5 lakh/year)
2. **Interest earned** is completely tax-free throughout the entire tenure
3. **Maturity amount** is completely tax-free

Put this in perspective. If you are in the 30% tax bracket and deposit Rs. 1.5 lakh every year, you save roughly Rs. 45,000 in taxes annually (plus cess). Over the 15-year deposit period, that is around Rs. 6.75 lakh saved in taxes alone -- on top of the 8.2% interest your money is earning.

No comparable product gives you this combination. Even PPF, which also has EEE status, gives you 1.1% less interest.

## Partial Withdrawal at 18 -- A Feature Many Parents Miss

This is genuinely one of the most useful parts of SSY that does not get talked about enough. When your daughter turns 18, she can withdraw up to 50% of the balance at the end of the previous financial year for higher education expenses. You just need to show an admission offer letter or fee receipt from the educational institution.

Think about it -- your daughter gets into a good college, you need money for fees and hostel, and you can pull out half the corpus without closing the account. The remaining amount keeps earning interest until the account matures. For families who have been diligently saving since their daughter was born, this can cover a big chunk of engineering, medical, or MBA fees.

The withdrawal can be taken as a lump sum or in installments over up to 5 years (one withdrawal per year).

## Maturity -- What Happens at 21

The account matures 21 years after it was opened. The full amount -- everything you deposited plus all the accumulated compound interest -- is paid to the girl. She can collect it herself once she is an adult.

If the account is not closed at maturity, the balance continues to earn interest at the prevailing SSY rate. But there is no more depositing -- the deposit window closes after 15 years.

Early closure is allowed after the girl turns 18 if she is getting married (one month before to three months after the wedding date). In unfortunate cases like the account holder's death or a life-threatening illness, premature closure is permitted with proper documentation.

## How Much Can You Actually Accumulate?

These are rough estimates at the current 8.2% rate (actual amounts will vary with quarterly rate changes):

**If you deposit Rs. 12,000 per year (Rs. 1,000/month) for 15 years:**
- Total deposits: Rs. 1,80,000
- Interest earned: Approximately Rs. 3,50,000
- **Maturity amount: Around Rs. 5,30,000**

**If you max it out at Rs. 1,50,000 per year for 15 years:**
- Total deposits: Rs. 22,50,000
- Interest earned: Approximately Rs. 43,50,000
- **Maturity amount: Around Rs. 66,00,000 (Rs. 66 lakh)**

Even the modest Rs. 1,000-a-month route nearly triples your money. Max it out and you are looking at a corpus that can fund higher education, professional courses, or give your daughter a strong financial start to her adult life.

## Account Transfer and Other Practicalities

You can transfer the SSY account from one post office to another, or between a post office and a bank, free of charge. Handy if you relocate for work. Just carry the passbook and your KYC documents to the new branch.

Two accounts maximum -- one per girl child. Exception: if you have twins or triplets in the second pregnancy, a third account is allowed with a medical certificate.

After the girl turns 18, she can operate the account herself. Until then, the parent or guardian manages it.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can the girl child open the account herself?**
No. A parent or legal guardian must open it. The girl can take over operations after she turns 18.

**What if we become NRIs after opening the account?**
If the girl child or parent ceases to be an Indian resident, the account must be closed. Otherwise, it will only earn the post office savings account rate (around 4%) instead of the SSY rate. This is a detail that catches NRI families off guard.

**Twin daughters and then another daughter -- can I open three accounts?**
Yes, with a medical certificate confirming the twins or triplets. Without that certificate, the maximum is two accounts.

**Can I have both SSY and PPF for the same child?**
Yes, they are separate schemes. But remember that the combined Section 80C deduction cap is Rs. 1.5 lakh across all eligible investments.
