## The Scheme Every Farmer Knows About

Walk into any village chai shop in UP, Bihar, or Madhya Pradesh and mention "do hazaar wali kisht" -- everybody knows exactly what you are talking about. PM-KISAN has become that deeply embedded in rural India. Launched on February 24, 2019, this scheme puts Rs. 6,000 per year into the bank accounts of landholding farmer families, split into three installments of Rs. 2,000 each.

The numbers are staggering. Over 11 crore farmer families have received money under PM-KISAN, and total disbursements have crossed Rs. 3 lakh crore. It started out covering only small and marginal farmers with up to 2 hectares, but the government quickly expanded it to all farmer families regardless of landholding size. That move brought in a lot more people -- and a lot more complications.

## When Does the Money Come?

Here is the installment schedule, though "usual release month" should be taken with a grain of salt -- delays of a few weeks are common:

| Installment | Period | Amount | Usual Release Month |
|-------------|--------|--------|-------------------|
| 1st Installment | April - July | Rs. 2,000 | April-June |
| 2nd Installment | August - November | Rs. 2,000 | August-October |
| 3rd Installment | December - March | Rs. 2,000 | December-February |

The money goes directly into the farmer's Aadhaar-linked bank account through DBT. No middleman, no leakage -- at least that is the design. In practice, if your Aadhaar is not properly seeded with your bank account, the payment simply fails and you end up running between the bank and the CSC centre trying to figure out what went wrong.

## The eKYC Headache

This is where things got messy for a lot of farmers. Starting 2022, the government made eKYC mandatory for all PM-KISAN beneficiaries. Miss the eKYC deadline, and your installment gets held up. Simple enough in theory.

But here is what happened on the ground: lakhs of farmers in remote areas had their Aadhaar linked to old mobile numbers -- numbers they no longer use or SIM cards that stopped working. The OTP-based eKYC requires the OTP to come on your Aadhaar-registered mobile number. If that number is dead, you are stuck. Many farmers had to first update their mobile number at the Aadhaar centre (which could be 30-40 km away in some places), then come back and do the eKYC.

Three ways to get eKYC done:
1. **OTP-based online:** Go to pmkisan.gov.in, click eKYC, enter your Aadhaar, get OTP on your Aadhaar-linked number. Takes two minutes -- if your mobile number is current.
2. **Biometric at CSC:** Visit the nearest Common Service Centre for fingerprint verification. This is the fallback for those with mobile number issues. Can get crowded during deadline periods.
3. **PM-KISAN App:** Download the app and complete OTP-based eKYC from your phone.

A quick tip -- if your Aadhaar mobile number is outdated, sort that out first before trying eKYC. Otherwise you will waste trips.

## Who Can and Cannot Get PM-KISAN

The eligibility is deceptively simple: you need to be a landholding farmer family. But the exclusion list catches many people off guard.

**You cannot get PM-KISAN if you are:**

1. An institutional landholder
2. A current or former MP, MLA, Minister, or Mayor
3. A government employee (central or state) -- except Group D/Multi-Tasking Staff
4. Someone who paid income tax in the last assessment year
5. A professional -- doctor, engineer, lawyer, CA, or architect registered with a professional body
6. A pensioner drawing Rs. 10,000 or more per month (again, Group D exempt)

That income tax exclusion trips up a lot of people. If a farmer's son files IT returns and the land is still in the father's name, it gets complicated. The definition of "farmer family" covers the farmer, their spouse, and minor children -- so if anyone in that unit paid income tax, the benefit can be denied.

## Registering for PM-KISAN -- Three Routes

### Do It Yourself Online

Go to [pmkisan.gov.in](https://pmkisan.gov.in), hit "New Farmer Registration," pick rural or urban, and fill in your Aadhaar, mobile number, bank details, and land details (survey number, khasra, khata). You will need to do the Aadhaar eKYC during registration itself.

The tricky part is the land details. Most farmers do not know their exact khasra or survey numbers off the top of their head. Keep your land documents -- khatauni, patta, or whatever your state calls it -- handy before you start.

### Through a CSC Centre

Take your Aadhaar, bank passbook, and land papers to the nearest CSC. The operator handles everything. This costs a small fee. For most farmers, especially those who are not comfortable with the internet, this is the practical route.

### Through the Village Patwari

In many villages, the Patwari is the first point of contact for anything land-related, and PM-KISAN registration is no different. Hand over your Aadhaar, bank passbook, and land documents to the Patwari -- they verify your land records and register you online. This method works well in remote areas where CSCs are far away or the internet is unreliable.

That said, some farmers have reported that Patwaris drag their feet or ask for "chai-paani." If you face this, you can always go the CSC or self-registration route instead.

## "Payment Failed" -- Now What?

The most common panic among PM-KISAN beneficiaries is seeing "Payment Failed" when they check their status. The usual culprits:

- **Aadhaar not linked to bank account:** This is the number one reason. Go to your bank and get it done -- it takes ten minutes.
- **Incorrect bank details:** Happens more often than you would think. Name mismatch between Aadhaar and bank records is a classic problem.
- **eKYC not completed:** No eKYC, no payment. Period.
- **Land verification pending:** Some states are slow in approving registrations at the district level.

To correct your details, go to pmkisan.gov.in and use the "Updation of Self Registration" option. For bank account changes, you will need to visit the CSC.

## What About State-Level Schemes?

Some states run their own farmer income support schemes alongside or instead of PM-KISAN:

- **Telangana's Rythu Bandhu** gives Rs. 10,000 per acre per year. This is separate from PM-KISAN, so Telangana farmers can technically get both. Rythu Bandhu is arguably more generous for farmers with larger landholdings.
- **Odisha's KALIA** scheme provides Rs. 10,000 per year to small and marginal farmers.
- **West Bengal's Krishak Bandhu** offers Rs. 10,000 per year, but the state has had an on-and-off relationship with PM-KISAN, and many Bengal farmers have had to choose one or the other.
- **Jharkhand** provides a state-level top-up for PM-KISAN beneficiaries.

## Quick FAQs

**Can tenant farmers apply?**
No. PM-KISAN is for landholders only. If you farm rented land and your name is not on the land records, you are not eligible. This has been a long-standing criticism of the scheme.

**My installment has been pending for months. What should I do?**
First, check your beneficiary status on the portal. Make sure eKYC is done, Aadhaar is linked to your bank account, and your bank details are correct. If everything looks fine from your end, reach out to the helpline at 155261 or 011-24300606. The helpline is often busy during installment release weeks, so keep trying.

**Can NRI farmers get PM-KISAN?**
If you own agricultural land in India and your name is on the land records, you may be eligible. But you need to meet all other criteria too.
