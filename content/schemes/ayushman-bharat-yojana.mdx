## Why Ayushman Bharat Matters More Than the Numbers Suggest

Before this scheme existed, a single hospital stay could wipe out a family's savings. It happened all the time -- a heart attack, a road accident, a cancer diagnosis, and suddenly the family was selling land, jewellery, or borrowing from moneylenders at criminal interest rates. About 6 crore Indians were pushed below the poverty line every year just because of medical expenses.

Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY, launched on September 23, 2018, tries to fix that. It gives Rs. 5 lakh of health coverage per year to over 12 crore families -- roughly 55 crore people. The treatment is cashless at empanelled hospitals. No upfront payment, no paperwork headache at the billing counter when you are already stressed about a family member's health.

One thing people get confused about: the Rs. 5 lakh cover is per family, not per person. So if one family member uses Rs. 3 lakh for a surgery, the remaining Rs. 2 lakh is what is left for the rest of the family for that year. Still, for families who had zero coverage before, this is a massive safety net.

## Who Gets Ayushman Bharat -- The SECC Problem

Here is where the scheme runs into real-world trouble. Eligibility is based on the Socio-Economic Caste Census (SECC) 2011 data. The government used that database to identify poor and vulnerable families. If your family was listed in SECC 2011 as deprived, you are in. If not, you are out.

The problem? SECC 2011 data is over a decade old. Families that have fallen into poverty since then are not covered. And some families that were listed in 2011 have improved their situation but still remain on the list. There is no easy mechanism to add newly poor families or remove those who no longer qualify.

**Rural eligibility** is based on deprivation markers like having a kutcha house, no adult earning member aged 16-59, being female-headed with no adult male, SC/ST household, or being landless and dependent on manual labour.

**Urban eligibility** covers specific occupational groups: rag pickers, domestic workers, street vendors, construction workers, rickshaw pullers, security guards, sweepers, shop assistants, and similar categories.

## Getting Your Ayushman Card

You do not need to run to any special government office. The e-card can be made right at any empanelled hospital. Here is how:

**At a hospital:** Walk into any PM-JAY empanelled hospital, find the Ayushman Mitra (there is usually a help desk near the entrance), show your Aadhaar card, get your fingerprint or iris scanned, and the e-card is generated on the spot. Done. Takes about 15 minutes on a good day.

**At a CSC centre:** Carry your Aadhaar and ration card. The operator checks your eligibility and prints the card. They may charge around Rs. 30 for this.

**Through the Ayushman App:** This has made things much easier recently. Download the app, register with your mobile number, complete Aadhaar-based verification, and download your digital card. It works at hospitals just like the physical card. For younger family members who are comfortable with phones, this is the fastest route.

## Checking If You Are Eligible

Before anything else, check your eligibility. Three ways:

1. Visit [mera.pmjay.gov.in](https://mera.pmjay.gov.in), enter your mobile number, get the OTP, and search by your name, ration card number, or RSBY URN.
2. Call **14555**. This toll-free helpline is actually quite responsive -- the executives check your eligibility using your Aadhaar or ration card details and give you a clear yes or no. We have tested it multiple times and the wait time is usually under 5 minutes.
3. Just walk into any empanelled hospital and ask the Ayushman Mitra to check.

If your name does not show up and you believe you should be eligible, it likely means your family was not captured in the SECC 2011 survey. Unfortunately, there is no appeal mechanism for this at the central level, though some states have added extra families under their own expanded lists.

## What Is Covered (and What Is Not)

PM-JAY covers over 1,350 medical packages across 26 specialties. The big ones:

| Specialty | Examples of Procedures |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Cardiology | Bypass surgery, Angioplasty, Valve replacement |
| Oncology | Chemotherapy, Radiation therapy, Cancer surgeries |
| Orthopedics | Knee replacement, Hip replacement, Spine surgery |
| Neurosurgery | Brain tumor surgery, Spinal cord surgery |
| Nephrology | Kidney transplant, Dialysis |
| Ophthalmology | Cataract surgery, Glaucoma treatment |
| General Surgery | Appendectomy, Hernia repair, Gallbladder surgery |
| Gynecology | Hysterectomy, Cesarean delivery |
| Emergency Care | Burns treatment, Polytrauma management |

Pre-existing conditions are covered from day one -- no waiting period. You also get 3 days of pre-hospitalization expenses and 15 days of post-hospitalization costs, including medicines and follow-up diagnostics. There is even a Rs. 250 transport allowance per hospitalization.

**Not covered:** OPD consultations, cosmetic procedures, fertility treatments, drug rehabilitation, and standalone diagnostic tests.

## Private Hospitals -- The Mixed Bag

Private hospitals have increasingly come on board with PM-JAY, and over 30,000 hospitals are now empanelled across India. But the experience varies. Some private hospitals treat Ayushman patients well; others quietly push them toward paid options, saying things like "the Ayushman rate does not cover this particular stent" or "you can upgrade to a better room by paying the difference." This is technically not allowed, but it happens.

If any empanelled hospital asks you to pay for a covered procedure, that is a violation. File a complaint on the PM-JAY portal or call 14555 immediately. The National Health Authority takes these complaints seriously.

Government hospitals, by contrast, process Ayushman claims more straightforwardly, though you may face longer queues and wait times.

## Portability Across States

One genuinely useful feature -- your Ayushman card works across India. If you are a migrant worker from Bihar living in Maharashtra and need surgery, you can walk into any empanelled hospital in Maharashtra and get treated. This portability has been a lifeline for the crores of people who migrate for work.

Some states like Rajasthan have enhanced the coverage to Rs. 25 lakh by merging their own scheme with PM-JAY. Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh, and Telangana have also integrated their state health schemes with the central program.

Worth noting: West Bengal, Delhi, and Odisha have not formally joined PM-JAY and run their own state schemes instead.

## Questions People Commonly Ask

**Do I have to pay any premium?**
Zero. The entire premium is paid by the central and state governments. It is completely free for the beneficiary.

**Is there a limit on how many times I can get hospitalized?**
No limit on the number of hospitalizations. But the total cover is Rs. 5 lakh per family per year. Whatever is not used does not carry forward to the next year -- the cover resets.

**I got treated at a hospital and they charged me. What do I do?**
Call 14555 or complain on the PM-JAY portal. Empanelled hospitals are strictly not allowed to charge beneficiaries for covered treatments. Keep your receipts as evidence.

**Can I get treatment outside my home state?**
Yes, at any empanelled hospital in any state. That is the portability feature.
