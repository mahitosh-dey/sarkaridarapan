## IBPS PO 2025 - Still One of the Safest Career Bets for Graduates

Banking jobs in India never go out of style. Your parents probably told you the same thing, and honestly, they're not entirely wrong. IBPS has released the PO 2025 notification with 4,455 vacancies across 11 public sector banks, and if you're a graduate looking for a stable career with a decent starting salary, don't miss this one.

A Probationary Officer starts at Rs. 36,000 basic pay, but the real picture is better than that number suggests. Add in DA, HRA (depends on your posting city), and other allowances, and your in-hand salary in the first year itself comes to around Rs. 52,000-56,000 depending on the location. You also get perks that private sector employees can only dream of - subsidised housing or quarters in many places, medical coverage for your entire family, concessional loans, and LFC for travel. Pension is now under NPS instead of the old pension scheme, which is a sore point for many, but the overall package is still very attractive.

Here's the part nobody talks about in coaching ads: your first posting as a PO will almost certainly be in a rural or semi-urban branch. That's just how it works. You'll spend 2-3 years there before you become eligible for a transfer to a bigger branch. Some people love the experience, some find it tough. Just go in with your eyes open.

## Who Can Apply

**Age:** You need to be between 20 and 30 as on 1st April 2025. SC/ST get 5 years relaxation, OBC (NCL) gets 3 years, PwD gets 10 years.

**Education:** Any graduation from a recognized university. Commerce, Arts, Science, Engineering - doesn't matter. Even final-year students can apply, but you need to have your passing certificate by the time you join. Keep that provisional degree ready.

## Exam Pattern - Know What You're Walking Into

### Prelims

| Section | Questions | Marks | Time |
|---------|-----------|-------|------|
| English Language | 30 | 30 | 20 minutes |
| Quantitative Aptitude | 35 | 35 | 20 minutes |
| Reasoning Ability | 35 | 35 | 20 minutes |
| **Total** | **100** | **100** | **60 minutes** |

Here's the thing about IBPS PO Prelims that catches people off guard: **sectional cutoffs**. It's not enough to score well overall. You need to clear the minimum marks in each section separately. A lot of otherwise strong candidates get knocked out because they scored 28 in Reasoning but got only 5 in English. Every section counts. 0.25 marks negative marking per wrong answer, so blind guessing is a bad strategy.

### Mains

| Section | Questions | Marks | Time |
|---------|-----------|-------|------|
| Reasoning & Computer Aptitude | 45 | 60 | 60 minutes |
| English Language | 35 | 40 | 40 minutes |
| Data Analysis & Interpretation | 35 | 60 | 45 minutes |
| General/Economy/Banking Awareness | 40 | 40 | 35 minutes |
| **Total** | **155** | **200** | **180 minutes** |

There's also a descriptive paper - 25 marks, 30 minutes - where you write a letter and an essay. This is where many candidates with decent typing skills gain an edge. Practice your typing speed beforehand because you're writing on a computer, not paper.

### Interview

100 marks. If you've cleared Mains, congratulations - you're already in the top few percent. The final merit is calculated as 80% Mains + 20% Interview. The interview is usually 15-20 minutes and they ask about your background, why banking, current banking news, and a few situational questions.

## How to Apply

1. Go to [ibps.in](https://www.ibps.in)
2. Find the CRP PO/MT-XV link
3. New users - register first with your basic details
4. Save your Registration Number and Password (write it down somewhere physical too)
5. Fill the application - personal info, education, work experience
6. Upload photo (4.5cm x 3.5cm) and signature (3.5cm x 2cm)
7. Choose up to 5 exam centre cities
8. Pay Rs. 850 (Rs. 175 for SC/ST/PwD) through debit card, credit card, net banking, or UPI
9. Submit and take a printout

## Preparation - What Actually Works

Forget about buying 15 different books. For Quant, RS Aggarwal is still the go-to for basics, then shift to sectional practice from any good online platform. Reasoning is all about practice - Puzzles and Seating Arrangement make up 60-70% of the reasoning section in Mains, so get comfortable with complex sets.

English is the section where most Hindi-medium aspirants struggle, and there's no sugarcoating it. Read an English newspaper for 30 minutes daily. The Hindu or Indian Express works best. This habit alone will improve your reading speed, vocabulary, and grammar over 3-4 months.

For Banking Awareness, follow RBI circulars, know the current repo rate, CRR, SLR, and recent policy changes. They ask very specific questions - "Which bank launched XYZ scheme?" kind of stuff. Monthly current affairs PDFs from Adda247 or GradeUp are enough for this.

Most importantly, take mock tests. Every week. Analyze every single one. This exam is as much about speed and time management as it is about knowledge. You get 20 minutes per section in Prelims - that's less than 40 seconds per question. If you haven't practiced under timed conditions, you'll freeze on exam day.
