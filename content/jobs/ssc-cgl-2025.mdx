## SSC CGL 2025 - The Exam Every Graduate Has Heard Of

If you're a graduate and you've ever thought about a government desk job, SSC CGL has probably crossed your mind at least once. This is big news for graduates - SSC has dropped the CGL 2025 notification with 14,582 vacancies across various central government ministries and departments. That's a solid number, and it's one of the highest in recent years.

Let's be honest about what we're dealing with here. CGL routinely pulls in 25-30 lakh applicants fighting for roughly 14,000 posts. The competition is brutal. Walk into any coaching centre in Mukherjee Nagar, Delhi, and you'll see thousands of aspirants buried in books, sharing PGs, and eating at dhabas because the rent alone eats up half their budget. That's the ground reality of CGL preparation.

Inspector posts in Income Tax and Excise are the sweet spot - decent number of seats, good salary, and the work profile is actually interesting.

## How to Apply

1. Head over to ssc.gov.in
2. New candidates need to do the One Time Registration first - keep your Aadhaar and a valid email handy
3. Log in with your credentials
4. Fill in the SSC CGL 2025 application form
5. Upload photo and signature (make sure the file sizes match the specifications, a lot of forms get rejected here)
6. Pay Rs. 100 online (women and SC/ST/ESM/PwD candidates don't pay anything)
7. Download the confirmation page and save the PDF somewhere safe

Pro tip: Don't wait till the last day. The SSC server crashes every single time the deadline approaches. Apply early and save yourself the headache.

## Exam Pattern - What Changed Recently

### Tier-I (Computer Based)
- Duration: 60 minutes
- Total Questions: 100
- Maximum Marks: 200
- Sections: General Intelligence, English Language, Quantitative Aptitude, General Awareness

Tier-I is basically a screening round. You need to clear it, but the real game is Tier-II.

### Tier-II (Computer Based)
- Paper I: Mathematical Abilities & Reasoning (390 marks)
- Paper II: English Language & Comprehension (390 marks)
- Paper III: Statistics (only for JSO aspirants)

Here's what many first-time candidates miss: the Tier-II pattern changed a couple of years ago and SSC merged Maths with Reasoning into one paper, and English became a separate paper of equal weightage. English used to be a small section that people could scrape through. Not anymore. If your English is weak, start working on it now. Seriously.

## The Real Talk on Preparation

Most toppers will tell you that 6-8 months of focused preparation is enough for CGL, and they're not wrong. But "focused" is doing a lot of heavy lifting in that sentence. We're talking 8-10 hours of daily study.

Maths is the backbone of this exam. If your calculations are slow, nothing else matters because you'll run out of time. Candidates who crack CGL typically practice 50-100 Maths questions every single day for months. Sounds extreme, but that's what it takes when the competition is this fierce.

For General Awareness, Lucent's GK is still the bible for most aspirants. Pair it with a monthly current affairs magazine and you're sorted. Reasoning is the most scoreable section if you learn the shortcuts - most coaching institutes in Mukherjee Nagar drill pattern recognition into students and it works.

English trips up a large chunk of Hindi-medium candidates. If that's you, start with basic grammar (Wren & Martin or SP Bakshi) and then move to reading comprehension. There's no shortcut here - you have to read.

Take mock tests religiously after your first 2-3 months of prep. Testbook, Adda247, or Oliveboard - pick any platform, but take at least 30-40 full-length mocks before D-day. Analysing where you're losing marks is more valuable than solving another set of questions.

## A Few Things Worth Knowing

Bihar and UP candidates typically dominate the applicant pool for CGL - the sheer volume of graduates from these states who apply is staggering. This also means zone-wise competition can vary. Candidates from the North-East or J&K sometimes benefit from lower cutoffs in certain categories.

Also, the document verification stage is strict. Make sure your graduation certificate, caste certificate (if applicable), and age proof are all in order. People have lost confirmed selections because of mismatches in names or dates across documents. Don't let paperwork be the reason you miss out after putting in months of hard work.
