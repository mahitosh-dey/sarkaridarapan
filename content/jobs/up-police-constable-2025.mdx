## UP Police Constable 2025 - 52,000 Vacancies Is Massive

52,000 posts. Even by Uttar Pradesh standards, that's a huge number. UPPRPB has officially announced the UP Police Constable Recruitment 2025 and if you're between 18-28, have passed 12th, and are reasonably fit, this is one opportunity you absolutely should not ignore.

UP Police constable recruitment always generates insane levels of interest. We're talking about India's most populous state, where government jobs are treated like gold dust. Expect 30-40 lakh applications for this one. The competition is intense, but 52,000 seats means your chances are much better here than in most other exams.

The starting salary at Pay Level 3 is Rs. 21,700 basic, which with DA and other allowances works out to roughly Rs. 30,000-33,000 in-hand for a fresh constable. That's a decent start, and government job perks - medical, pension, job security - make it worthwhile for the long run.

## Eligibility - Check Before You Apply

**Age:** 18 to 28 as on 1st July 2025. SC/ST of UP get 5 years relaxation, OBC of UP gets 3 years, PwD gets 15 years. Note: the relaxation is for UP domicile candidates only.

**Education:** 12th pass (Intermediate) from any recognized board. That's it. Distance education and open university degrees are accepted too, as long as the university has UGC recognition.

**Domicile:** You must be a citizen of India AND a domicile of Uttar Pradesh. Your domicile certificate is non-negotiable.

### Physical Standards - Read This Carefully

**Males:**
- Height: 168 cm (General/OBC/SC), 160 cm (ST)
- Chest: 79 cm normal, 84 cm expanded (minimum 5 cm expansion)

**Females:**
- Height: 152 cm (General/OBC/SC), 147 cm (ST)
- Weight: Proportionate to height

These are hard cutoffs. If you're even 1 cm short, you're out. No exceptions. Candidates sometimes show up thinking there's some flexibility here - there isn't.

## Selection Process - Four Stages, Each One Can Eliminate You

### Stage 1: Written Exam (CBT)

| Subject | Questions | Marks |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| General Hindi | 37 | 74 |
| General Knowledge / Current Affairs | 38 | 76 |
| Numerical & Mental Ability | 38 | 76 |
| Mental Aptitude / IQ / Reasoning | 37 | 74 |
| **Total** | **150** | **300** |

- 2 hours to complete
- 0.5 marks deducted per wrong answer
- All MCQ, available in Hindi and English

The written exam is objective type and honestly, it's quite doable if you put in 3-4 months of solid practice. The difficulty is moderate - think state-level competitive exam, not SSC or banking level. General Hindi carries good marks and most UP candidates are comfortable with it, which is an advantage.

For GK, focus heavily on UP-specific questions - districts, rivers, folk art, festivals, historical monuments, famous personalities from UP. They ask a surprising number of state-level GK questions. National and international current affairs also feature, but UP GK is your low-hanging fruit.

Numerical ability is basic - percentage, ratio, profit-loss, simple interest, time and work. Nothing from advanced maths. If you've done Class 10 maths properly, you just need practice for speed.

Reasoning is straightforward too - coding-decoding, series, analogies, blood relations, direction sense. Solve 30-40 questions daily and you'll be well-prepared within 2 months.

### Stage 2: Physical Standard Test (PST)

After the written exam results, qualified candidates are called for PST. They measure your height and chest on the spot. This is pass/fail - either you meet the standards or you don't.

### Stage 3: Physical Efficiency Test (PET) - The Real Bottleneck

**Males:** 4.8 km run in 25 minutes
**Females:** 2.4 km run in 14 minutes

This is where the real elimination happens. The written exam filters out maybe 60-70% of candidates, but the physical test knocks out a huge chunk of those who remain. 4.8 km in 25 minutes sounds easy on paper, but try doing it on a hot August day in UP, on a ground that might not be a proper track, with hundreds of other candidates running alongside you. It's tough.

Start running NOW if you haven't already. Not tomorrow, not next week - today. Build up gradually. Run 2 km daily for the first two weeks, then increase to 3 km, then 4 km, and work on your timing. On test day, you need to finish 4.8 km in under 25 minutes, which means roughly a 5:12 per km pace. That's a brisk jog, not a sprint, but sustaining it for nearly 5 km requires stamina you can't build in the last two weeks.

### Stage 4: Document Verification - Be Prepared

This is where things get strict. UP Police document verification is known for being thorough, and candidates get rejected at this stage more often than you'd expect. Keep the following ready well in advance:

- 10th and 12th marksheets and certificates (originals + photocopies)
- Date of birth proof (10th certificate)
- UP domicile certificate
- Caste certificate (if applicable) - get it from the Tehsildar's office, not some random agent
- EWS certificate (if applicable)
- Character certificate
- Aadhaar Card
- Recent passport size photographs

Make sure your name, father's name, and date of birth match EXACTLY across all documents. Even a minor spelling difference (like "Mohammad" vs "Mohammed" or "Kumar" vs "Kumari") can create problems. Get corrections done beforehand if there are any discrepancies.

### Stage 5: Medical Exam

Final step. They check eyesight (6/6 in better eye, 6/9 in worse eye, without glasses), hearing, and general physical fitness. Colour blindness is a disqualification.

## How to Apply

1. Visit [uppbpb.gov.in](https://uppbpb.gov.in)
2. Click on the "UP Police Constable Recruitment 2025" link
3. Hit "Apply Online" or "New Registration"
4. Enter basic details - name, father's name, DOB, mobile, email
5. You'll get a registration number and password via SMS and email
6. Log in and fill the complete form - personal details, education, address
7. Upload photo (passport size, 20-50 KB, JPG), signature (10-20 KB), and thumb impression
8. Select your preferred exam district
9. Pay Rs. 400 (Rs. 200 for SC/ST) via net banking, debit card, credit card, or UPI
10. Review everything, submit, and take a printout

## A Word About Paper Leaks and Re-exams

Let's address the elephant in the room. UP Police recruitment has had a troubled history with paper leaks and cancelled exams. The 2018 constable exam was cancelled and re-conducted. Candidates spent months in uncertainty. While the government has taken steps to improve security - CCTV in exam centres, jammers, and stricter protocols - you should be mentally prepared for delays or rescheduling. Keep following the official website and don't rely on rumours circulating on WhatsApp or Telegram groups. If something changes, UPPRPB will announce it on uppbpb.gov.in.

## Preparation Resources That Work

- **General Hindi:** Lucent's Samanya Hindi or any standard UP Board Hindi grammar book
- **GK:** Lucent's GK + UP GK section from Upkar or Kiran Prakashan + monthly current affairs
- **Numerical Ability:** RS Aggarwal (Arithmetic section only) or Kiran Prakashan maths book
- **Reasoning:** RS Aggarwal Verbal & Non-Verbal Reasoning
- **Physical:** Daily running routine, starting at least 3 months before PET

Mock tests are your best friend for the written exam. Testbook, Adda247, and Gradeup all have UP Police-specific test series. Take at least 20-25 full mocks before exam day.
