Joining the Indian Armed Forces was always the dream. But if you graduated past 2022, the way you get in has changed. The Agnipath Scheme replaced the old direct recruitment model — and Agniveer is the name given to everyone recruited under it.

In 2026, all three services — **Indian Army, Indian Navy, and Indian Air Force** — have released their Agniveer batches. The vacancies are real, the salary is real, and the service is four years. If you've been on the fence about whether this is worth it, this guide will give you everything you need to make that call.

Let's go service by service, and then we'll compare all three side by side.

---

## Indian Army Agniveer 2026

The Army is the biggest recruiter under Agnipath. The 2026 batch has **over 25,000 vacancies** across five categories — from 8th pass to 10+2 level. If you've been waiting for the Army to call you, this is it.

### Categories and Minimum Qualification

| Category | Minimum Qualification | Key Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Agniveer General Duty (GD) | 10th pass | 45% aggregate, 33% in each subject |
| Agniveer Technical | 10+2 Science (PCM) | 50% aggregate in Physics, Chemistry, Maths & English |
| Agniveer Clerk / Store Keeper Technical | 10+2 any stream | 60% aggregate, 50% in English — compulsory |
| Agniveer Tradesman (10th) | 10th pass | 33% marks |
| Agniveer Tradesman (8th) | 8th pass | Cook, Washer Man, Safai Karamchari roles |

GD is the most popular category and the one most people apply for. But don't ignore Technical and Clerk/SKT — competition is lower and the roles are often more stable and indoor.

### Age Limit

**17½ to 24 years** as on the date of enrolment. No relaxation for any category.

The Common Entrance Exam (CBT) is a computer-based objective test. If you're appearing this week, keep reading for the exam pattern below.

## Indian Navy Agniveer 2026

The Navy recruits under two entries: **SSR** for 10+2 qualified candidates and **MR** for 10th pass candidates. Both men and women can apply, and there is **no application fee** — which is unusual among government exam recruitments.

The 2026 batch had approximately **3,000 vacancies** across SSR and MR.

### SSR vs MR — What's the Difference?

| Entry | Full Name | Qualification | Trades |
|---|---|---|---|
| Agniveer SSR | Senior Secondary Recruit | 10+2 with Maths & Physics + one of Chem/Bio/CS | Seamanship, Electrical, Engineering, Communications, Medical Assistant |
| Agniveer MR | Matric Recruit | 10th pass | Chef, Steward, Hygienist |

SSR gets you into technical and operations roles — the core of what sailors actually do on ships. MR is support services. If you have the 10+2 qualification with the right subjects, always go for SSR.

### Eligibility

**Age:** 17½ to 21 years. Both male and female Indian citizens can apply.

**SSR Qualification:**
10+2 (passed or appearing) with:
- Mathematics and Physics — compulsory
- Plus any one of: Chemistry, Biology, or Computer Science
- From a Board of School Education recognised by the Ministry of Education

**MR Qualification:**
10th class pass from a recognised board. No specific subject requirement.

**Marital Status:** Must be unmarried. Cannot marry during the 4-year service.

### Important Dates — 2026 Batch

| Event | Date |
|---|---|
| Application Opens | March 14, 2026 |
| Application Closes | April 6, 2026 |
| Application Fee | **Nil** |
| Agniveer SSR Written Exam | May 13, 2026 |
| Agniveer MR Written Exam | May 14–15, 2026 |
| Physical Fitness Test | After written results |
| Training Commencement | December 2026 (tentative) |

Both SSR and MR exams for the 2026 batch have been conducted. Results and PFT schedules will be updated on joinindiannavy.gov.in.

### Physical Fitness Test

| Test | Standard |

| Push-ups | 10 |

Navy's PFT run time (7 minutes) is more relaxed than the Army's (5 min 30 sec). But don't take it lightly — candidates who show up underprepared still fail.

### Training

Selected Agniveers report to **INS Chilka, Odisha** for basic naval training — 14 to 16 weeks. This is where you transition from civilian to sailor. After basic training, you go to specialist training at Naval establishments based on your trade.

From Day 1 of training, you're paid the full monthly package.

## Indian Air Force Agniveer Vayu 2026

The Air Force's Agniveer entry is called **Agniveer Vayu**. The 01/2026 batch had approximately **2,500 vacancies**. The Air Force has one requirement that the Army and Navy don't emphasise the same way — you need at least **50% marks specifically in English**. Not just aggregate, but English as a separate subject. Many candidates who meet the aggregate cut-off still get rejected because their English marks are below 50%.

### Trade Groups

| Group | Qualification | Subjects Required |
|---|---|---|
| Science (PCM) | 10+2 | Physics, Maths, English — 50% aggregate & 50% in English |
| Science (Other / PCB) | 10+2 | Biology, Chemistry, English — 50% aggregate & 50% in English |
| Other (Non-Science) | 10+2 any stream | Any subjects including English — 50% aggregate & 50% in English |

Your trade group determines your role in the IAF. PCM candidates are placed in technical trades (electronics, avionics, mechanical, electrical). Non-science candidates go into ground support roles (accounts, education, administrative, logistics).

### Age Limit

**17½ to 21 years.** For the 01/2026 batch: born between **July 3, 2004 and January 3, 2008**.

Only unmarried Indian citizens — both male and female — are eligible.

## Salary — Same Across All Three Services

The monthly package under Agnipath is standardised — Army, Navy, and Air Force all pay the same:

| Year | Monthly Package | In-Hand Pay | Contribution to Seva Nidhi |
|---|---|---|---|
| Year 1 | ₹30,000 | ₹21,000 | ₹9,000 |
| Year 2 | ₹33,000 | ₹23,100 | ₹9,900 |
| Year 3 | ₹36,500 | ₹25,550 | ₹10,950 |
| Year 4 | ₹40,000 | ₹28,000 | ₹12,000 |

**Seva Nidhi at the end of 4 years: ₹10.04 lakh** — fully tax-free. This is your contribution plus an equal government matching amount, with interest.

In addition to the monthly package, all Agniveers receive:
- Risk and Hardship allowance
- Dress allowance
- Travel allowance
- Medical care throughout the service period
- Free ration, accommodation, and clothing during training

**Life insurance (Army): ₹48 lakh cover during service.** In case of death during service, a death grant of ₹44 lakh is paid to the family (non-battle casualty). Battle casualty benefits are higher.

## What Happens After 4 Years?

This is the question everyone asks, and it deserves an honest answer.

After 4 years of service:

**25% are permanently retained** — selected by each service based on performance, discipline, and merit during the 4-year tenure. These retained Agniveers become regular soldiers/sailors/airmen with full service benefits, pension, and a long-term career in uniform.

**75% are released** with:
- ₹10.04 lakh Seva Nidhi (tax-free in hand)
- A skill certificate recognised by the government
- For Army: a Class 12 equivalent education certificate after completing a course during service
- Priority in Central Armed Police Forces (CRPF, BSF, CISF, ITBP, SSB) recruitment
- A 10% reservation in CAPFs and Assam Rifles (notified by the Ministry of Home Affairs)
- Many state governments have announced reservation in police and government jobs for Agniveer alumni

Will every state honour these commitments? That depends on government policy at the time of your exit. But the central government's promises — CAPF reservation and skill certificates — are legally notified.

## Which Service Should You Apply For?

Here's the honest breakdown:

**Apply for the Army if:**
- You're between 17½ and 24 years (wider age window than Navy/Air Force)
- You passed 8th, 10th, or 12th — the Army has categories for every qualification level
- You want the biggest batch with the most vacancies
- You're aiming for infantry, armoured corps, or ground combat roles

**Apply for the Navy if:**
- You're 10th or 10+2 pass and want to be on ships
- You want to save the ₹250 application fee (Navy has no fee)
- You're interested in technical trades at sea
- You want shorter run-time standard on the PFT (7 minutes vs Army's 5:30)

**Apply for the Air Force if:**
- You have a science background and are genuinely interested in aviation support
- Your English marks in 12th are 50% or above — this is non-negotiable for IAF
- You want to work in electronics, avionics, or ground support for aircraft
- You're comfortable with a smaller batch (2,500 vacancies vs Army's 25,000+)

## Preparation Tips

### For CBT / Written Test

The exam is not hard if you've genuinely studied through school. Here's what matters:

1. **Revise your Class 10 and 12 NCERT textbooks first.** The GD and MR papers are mostly Class 9–10 level. Technical and SSR papers go up to Class 12. Don't buy expensive coaching material before finishing the basics.

2. **Solve previous year papers.** Army, Navy, and Air Force all repeat question types. Even if the exact questions change, the format and difficulty level stay consistent. You'll find PYQs on each service's official website.

3. **English is a scoring section — don't skip it.** Many candidates from Hindi-medium schools underestimate English. The Air Force makes 50% in English a hard cut-off. Use basic grammar books and practice comprehension passages daily.

4. **Reasoning and GK need regular reading.** For GK, read any standard current affairs booklet from the last 6 months. Focus on defence news, national events, and basic history/geography.

5. **Negative marking is 0.25 — don't over-guess.** If you have no idea about a question, leave it. But if you can eliminate even one option, attempt it.

### For Physical Fitness Test

The written test gets you in the door. The PFT decides if you walk through it.

- Start running 1.6 km every day, 8 to 10 weeks before the exam
- Don't just run — time yourself. Your goal is to beat 5:30 for Army, 6:30 for Air Force, and 7 minutes for Navy
- Do push-ups and pull-ups every morning. Start from whatever you can manage today and add one rep every three days
- Don't start hard physical training the week before the PFT. You need to be rested, not sore

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can girls apply for Agniveer in all three services?**
Yes. Indian Army, Navy, and Air Force all accept female candidates for Agniveer recruitment. However, specific roles within each service may have additional physical or medical standards.

**Q: Is Agniveer a permanent government job?**
No. It is a 4-year contractual military service. Only 25% of Agniveers in each batch are retained permanently at the end of 4 years. The rest exit with the Seva Nidhi package and other benefits.

**Q: Can I apply for more than one service at the same time?**
Yes. You can apply for Army, Navy, and Air Force simultaneously if the application windows overlap and you meet the eligibility for each. This maximises your chances.

**Q: What is Seva Nidhi and is it really tax-free?**
Seva Nidhi is the exit payment after 4 years. It totals ₹10.04 lakh — made up of your monthly contributions during service plus an equal matching amount by the Government, with interest. It is fully exempt from income tax as per the Finance Act, 2022.

**Q: I failed the 2025 Army rally. Can I apply for the 2026 CBT?**
Yes, provided you still meet the age limit. There is no bar on reapplying after failing a previous attempt.

**Q: What happens if I fail medically during the 4-year service?**
You are discharged from service on medical grounds. Compensation and benefits depend on whether the disability was attributable to service. The Armed Forces have specific regulations governing medical discharge compensation.
