Not a single central government exam in India bars women from applying. Not UPSC, not SSC, not IBPS, not Railways, not the Defence exams. The playing field is level — and in some cases, it actively tilts in women's favour with reserved seats, fee waivers, and dedicated recruitment drives.

If you're a woman looking for a government job in 2026, the question is not "can I apply?" — you almost certainly can. The question is "which exam gives me the best shot given my qualification, location, and what I actually want to do?"

This guide answers that, category by category.

## 1. SSC — Most Women-Friendly Central Exam

The Staff Selection Commission runs the largest central government recruitment in India. Here's a fact most people don't know: **SSC waives the application fee for all women candidates across every SSC exam** — CGL, CHSL, MTS, CPO, and GD Constable. Male candidates pay ₹100–₹200 per exam. Women pay nothing.

### SSC CGL (Combined Graduate Level)

**Who can apply:** Any graduate (arts, science, commerce, engineering — doesn't matter)
**What you can become:** Income Tax Inspector, CBI Sub-Inspector, Enforcement Officer, Statistical Investigator, Auditor, Accountant, Assistant Section Officer in various ministries

**SSC CGL 2026 — ACTIVE RIGHT NOW:**
The SSC CGL 2026 notification is out with **12,256 vacancies**. Application is open till **June 22, 2026** at ssc.gov.in. Tier 1 exam is in August–September 2026. If you're a graduate and haven't applied yet, this is your most immediate action item.

**Salary:** Income Tax Inspector — ₹65,000–₹75,000 per month in metro cities (Pay Level 7, ₹44,900–₹1,42,400 base). Auditor/Accountant — ₹50,000–₹60,000 per month.

**Selection:** Tier 1 (online MCQ) → Tier 2 (online MCQ, now with sectional timing) → Document Verification

### SSC CHSL (Combined Higher Secondary Level)

**Who can apply:** Class 12 pass
**What you can become:** Postal Assistant, Sorting Assistant, Lower Division Clerk (LDC), Data Entry Operator

**Salary:** ₹25,000–₹35,000 in-hand (Pay Level 4 and 5)

CHSL is one of the most accessible central government jobs — 12th pass is enough, the exam is manageable, and the work is largely office-based in a government setting.

### SSC GD Constable

**Who can apply:** 10th pass, age 18–23
**What you can become:** Constable in CISF, CRPF, BSF, ITBP, SSB, NIA, Secretariat Security Force

**Physical Standards for Women (GD):**
- Height: 157 cm (relaxed for hill areas and North-East: 150 cm)
- Race: 1.6 km in 8 minutes 30 seconds (compared to 6 minutes for men)
- Weight: Proportionate to height

**Salary:** ₹23,000–₹30,000 in-hand + free accommodation, rations, medical

## 2. Banking — Fixed Hours, Good Salary, Transfers Every 3–5 Years

Public sector banking is one of the most financially rewarding government-linked careers for women in India. The 12th Bipartite Settlement (2025–26) has revised banking salaries significantly upward.

### IBPS PO (Probationary Officer)

**Eligibility:** Any graduation, age 20–30
**Salary (after 12th Bipartite):** ₹50,000–₹65,000 in-hand for new joinees, growing to ₹85,000+ at Scale II
**Selection:** Prelims → Mains → Interview
**IBPS PO 2026:** Notification typically released July–August each year

### IBPS Clerk

**Eligibility:** Any graduation, age 20–28
**Salary:** ₹35,000–₹45,000 in-hand
**Key benefit:** Clerk posts are often posted in home state or home region — important if you need to stay in your city

### SBI PO and SBI Clerk

SBI runs its own exams independently of IBPS. Higher salary than IBPS, more competitive, but also more prestigious. SBI PO salary starts at ₹65,000+ in-hand.

### RBI Grade B

**Eligibility:** Graduation with 60% (SC/ST 55%), age up to 30
**Salary:** ₹1,05,000–₹1,30,000 per month (the highest-paying government banking job)
**Selection:** Phase I (online) → Phase II (descriptive + objective) → Interview
This is the most competitive banking exam in India. Expect 1–2 years of dedicated preparation.

## 3. Railway — Huge Organisation, Stable Jobs, Women-Specific Quota in RPF

Indian Railways is one of the largest employers in the world. For women, the key openings are:

### Railway NTPC (Non-Technical Popular Categories)

**Eligibility:** Graduation or 12th pass depending on the post
**Posts:** Station Master, Goods Guard, Commercial cum Ticket Clerk, Junior Account Assistant, Senior Commercial cum Ticket Clerk
**Salary:** ₹35,000–₹75,000 depending on level
**Selection:** CBT 1 → CBT 2 → Skill Test / Typing Test → Medical

### RPF — Railway Protection Force (Female Constable / Sub-Inspector)

**15% of RPF vacancies are reserved for women.** This is a Central Armed Police Force role — you're protecting railway premises and passengers.

- **Constable:** 10th pass, age 18–28, physical fitness test required
- **Sub-Inspector:** Graduation, age 20–28

**Physical standards for women RPF:**
- Height: 157 cm (relaxed for North-East and hill regions: 152 cm)
- 1.6 km run: within 8 minutes

**Salary:** RPF Constable ₹23,000–₹30,000 + uniform allowance, accommodation, medical
RPF SI ₹40,000–₹55,000 in-hand

## 4. UPSC Civil Services — IAS, IPS, IFS

**Eligibility:** Any graduation, age 21–32 (General/EWS)
**Vacancies 2026:** 933 posts across IAS, IPS, IFS, and central services
**Prelims:** May 24, 2026 (done) | **Mains:** August 21, 2026

UPSC is purely merit-based — no gender reservation. Women compete on the same merit list as men. In recent years, women have been consistently among the top rankers. In UPSC 2022, the top 3 positions were all held by women.

This is the highest-prestige government career available in India. IAS and IPS officers have real decision-making power over administration, law enforcement, and policy. The path is long (12–18 months of preparation), but for those who clear it, no other government career comes close in terms of impact and authority.

## 5. Teaching — Most Accessible for B.Ed Graduates

Teaching in central government schools is a stable, well-paying, and location-friendly career. The three main pathways:

### KVS (Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan)

**Eligibility:** Graduation + B.Ed + CTET qualified
**Salary:** Primary Teacher ₹35,000–₹45,000 | TGT ₹45,000–₹55,000 | PGT ₹55,000–₹70,000
**KVS runs its own recruitment** — watch for notifications at kvsangathan.nic.in

### NVS (Navodaya Vidyalaya Samiti)

Similar to KVS but for Navodaya Vidyalayas. Good options for teachers in rural and semi-urban areas. Recruitment at navodaya.gov.in.

### CTET + State Teaching

CTET qualifies you for central government school jobs (KVS, NVS, Army schools, central institutions). Many states also accept CTET for their state school appointments. CTET certificate is now **valid for a lifetime** (changed from 7 years in 2021).

## 6. State Police — 20–33% Women Reservation in Most States

State police recruitment varies by state, but most Indian states have implemented specific women's reservation:
- Rajasthan, Maharashtra, Karnataka: 33% for women in constable posts
- Uttar Pradesh: 20% for women in constable posts
- Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand, and other hill states: 30%+

State police is often the most accessible government job with a physical component for women at the 10th or 12th pass level. Check your state police's official website for the current recruitment cycle.

**Physical standards differ by state** — the run distance and timing are generally more relaxed than Army/CRPF standards.

## 7. Defence — Agniveer + Officer Entries

### Agniveer (Soldiers)

All three services — Army, Navy, Air Force — accept women in their Agniveer recruitment.
- **Army:** Agniveer (all categories including GD, Technical, Clerk/SKT)
- **Navy:** Agniveer SSR and MR
- **Air Force:** Agniveer Vayu

4-year contractual service, ₹21,000–₹28,000 monthly in-hand, Seva Nidhi of ₹10.04 lakh tax-free at exit. 25% retention as permanent soldiers.

### Officer Entries (Long-term career)

Women can become officers in the Indian Army via:
- Short Service Commission (SSC) — JAG Entry (Law), AEC Entry (Education), AMC (Army Medical Corps)
- Women Military Police — opened in 2021
- NDA — opened to women from 2022 onwards

In the Air Force: women can fly as fighter pilots (Flying Branch) — a historic change from 2016 onwards. Many women pilots are now serving in the IAF.

## Practical Tips

**Apply to SSC exams first if you haven't already.** The fee waiver for women means there's no downside to applying for all SSC exams you're eligible for. SSC CGL 2026 closes June 22 — apply today if you're a graduate.

**Don't skip Banking because of the maths section.** Banking prelims quantitative aptitude is manageable with 60–90 days of focused practice. Many women who "hated maths in school" have cleared IBPS PO — it's application-level arithmetic, not engineering maths.

**Physical fitness exams need 3 months of training minimum.** For police, RPF, GD Constable, or Agniveer, start running and doing basic exercises 3 full months before the physical test date. One month is not enough.

**CTET is a lifetime qualification.** If you're a B.Ed graduate, appear in CTET even if you're targeting other jobs. It costs ₹1,000–₹1,200 and qualifies you for all central government teaching jobs forever.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Are there government jobs reserved only for women?**
Some posts like Women Constable in state police and Women Home Guard are exclusively for women. For central government jobs, most posts are open to all genders with specific % seats reserved for women (like 15% in RPF, 33% in some state police forces). Most central exams like SSC, UPSC, Banking, and Railways are purely merit-based with no gender quota.

**Q: Do physical tests apply to all government jobs?**
No. Most government jobs — SSC, Banking, Railways (non-RPF), Teaching, UPSC — have no physical fitness test. Physical tests apply to Police, RPF, Defence (Agniveer and officer entries), and CISF/CRPF/BSF constable roles.

**Q: Can women appear for NDA 2026?**
Yes. Since September 2022, women can appear for the National Defence Academy (NDA) examination. This opens the path to becoming permanent commissioned officers in the Indian Army, Navy, and Air Force straight after Class 12.

**Q: Is there an age relaxation for women in government exams?**
Most central government exams don't offer gender-specific age relaxation. The standard category relaxations (SC/ST, OBC, Ex-serviceman, PwBD) apply to women and men equally. Some states offer additional age relaxation for women — check your state's specific recruitment rules.
