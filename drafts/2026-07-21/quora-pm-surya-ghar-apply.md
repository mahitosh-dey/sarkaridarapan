## Draft answer (paste into Quora)

Before anything else, one update most existing answers on this thread are missing. MNRE paused fresh registrations on pmsuryaghar.gov.in from 6 June 2026, and no formal reopening date has been announced as of July 2026. If you already registered before 6 June, your installation, DISCOM inspection, net metering, and subsidy payout continue as normal. If you did not register, you cannot submit a new application right now.

Two practical steps in the meantime. First, keep a scanned bundle of your latest electricity bill, Aadhaar, and cancelled cheque ready so you can register in minutes when the portal reopens. Second, do not sign a contract with any vendor who claims they can enrol you offline while the portal is closed. Subsidy is disbursed only against portal-registered applications, and any offline promise is a red flag.

Now the actual application flow, which applies both to existing pre-6-June batches and to whoever registers after the portal reopens.

**Step-by-step on pmsuryaghar.gov.in**

Stage 1 is registration with your state, DISCOM name, and consumer number from your electricity bill.

Stage 2 is Aadhaar-linked household verification with OTP.

Stage 3 is roof feasibility declaration where you upload a rooftop photograph and enter approximate roof area in square feet.

Stage 4 is DISCOM feasibility approval. This is the single biggest variable and ranges from 7 days in Gujarat to 30-plus days in Bihar and the northeast.

Stage 5 is empanelled vendor selection from the portal's state-wise list. Contract signing and site survey happen after selection.

Stage 6 is installation, with a target completion of 30 to 45 days after site survey.

Stage 7 is DISCOM net metering inspection followed by subsidy disbursement to your registered bank account within 30 days.

End-to-end runs 90 to 120 days in Gujarat and Maharashtra, 105 to 120 in Kerala and Karnataka, and 135 to 150-plus in West Bengal, Bihar, and northeastern states.

**Documents to keep ready**

Aadhaar card, latest electricity bill in the applicant's name (this is important, the Aadhaar name must match the bill account holder), cancelled cheque of the bank account you want subsidy disbursed to, and a rooftop photograph. If you live in group housing, you also need society-level resolution for RWA applications and DISCOM approval for a common connection.

Tenants cannot apply because the electricity connection must be in the applicant's name. If you rent, either the landlord applies or you work out a tripartite arrangement, which most vendors avoid due to liability.

**Subsidy math and what a 3 kW system actually costs**

Vendor quotes in July 2026 for a 3 kW mono-PERC system range from Rs 1.62 lakh to Rs 1.95 lakh installed. Central subsidy is Rs 30,000 per kW for the first 2 kW and Rs 18,000 for the 3rd kW, capping at Rs 78,000 for a 3 kW system.

Taking the mid-point of Rs 1.80 lakh gross cost, your net out-of-pocket after the Rs 78,000 subsidy is about Rs 1.02 lakh. If your pre-solar monthly bill was Rs 2,500 and the system takes it to zero, payback works out to roughly 3 years 5 months. A 25-year panel warranty means about 21 years of near-free electricity after payback.

If you cannot pay Rs 1.02 lakh upfront, MNRE has partnered with major PSBs for collateral-free rooftop solar loans up to Rs 2 lakh, and hypothecated loans up to Rs 6 lakh. Rates in July 2026 are 7.15 percent at SBI, 7.30 percent at Canara Bank, 7.35 percent at Union Bank and Bank of Baroda. Applications route through jansamarth.in.

**One more thing to check**

Only apply through vendors listed on the portal's Vendor Search by state and district. If a vendor approaches you offline and claims empanelment without a matching portal entry, walk away. Subsidy disbursement is tied to the specific portal-registered vendor linked to your application.

Longer breakdown with state-wise DISCOM timelines, bank loan comparison table, and RWA rules if you want the full reference: https://www.sarkaridarapan.com/sarkari-yojana/pm-surya-ghar-muft-bijli-yojana-rooftop-solar-2026

Primary sources: pmsuryaghar.gov.in official portal, mnre.gov.in Ministry site, jansamarth.in loan portal.
