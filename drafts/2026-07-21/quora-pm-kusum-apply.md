## Draft answer (paste into Quora)

Most first-time applicants make one mistake: they open pmkusum.mnre.gov.in and try to apply there. That is the national dashboard for tracking progress, not the actual application portal. PM-KUSUM applications are handled by your state renewable energy agency, and each state has its own portal.

Here is where you actually apply based on your state.

- Rajasthan: pmkusum.rajasthan.gov.in (RRECL)
- Maharashtra: pmkusum.maharashtra.gov.in (MEDA)
- Uttar Pradesh: upneda.org.in for Component B, upnedakusumc2.in for Component C2
- Haryana: hareda.gov.in (fastest processing right now, 69 percent target completion)
- Madhya Pradesh: mpuvnl.mp.gov.in
- Gujarat: geda.gujarat.gov.in
- Andhra Pradesh: apreda.gov.in
- Karnataka: kredl.karnataka.gov.in

If your state is not on this list, search for your state nodal agency name plus "PM-KUSUM" and you will land on the right portal.

**Documents to keep ready before opening the form**

Aadhaar card, PM Kisan number if you have one, land ownership record in the format your state uses (7/12 extract in Maharashtra, jamabandi in Punjab, khasra khatauni in UP and MP, patta in Tamil Nadu, RTC in Karnataka), bank passbook copy for subsidy disbursal, cancelled cheque, and a bore well test certificate showing water availability at your pump depth. The bore well certificate is where many applications get stuck because the state ground water department can take 15 to 30 days to issue it. Get this started first.

**How the subsidy math works for Component B (standalone solar pump)**

For a general farmer, subsidy is 60 percent of the pump benchmark cost. 30 percent central and 30 percent state. Farmer share is 40 percent. For SC, ST, small, and marginal farmers in most states, farmer share drops to 10 percent because the state adds a 30 percent top-up.

A 5 HP pump at the Maharashtra benchmark of about Rs 2.85 lakh works out to Rs 1.14 lakh farmer share for a general farmer, and Rs 28,500 for an SC or ST farmer. A 3 HP pump at about Rs 1.80 lakh benchmark works out to Rs 72,000 general farmer share, Rs 18,000 SC or ST. The state nodal agency releases the subsidy directly to the empanelled vendor after installation and DISCOM verification, so you do not need to chase it separately.

You can pay the farmer share upfront or take a bank loan at 7 to 8 percent per annum through a referral from the state nodal agency. State Bank of India, Bank of Baroda, and Canara Bank have PM-KUSUM linked loan products. If you already hold a Kisan Credit Card at a cooperative bank, a PM-KUSUM loan can often be sanctioned in about a week using your existing KCC file.

**Timeline expectations by state**

Haryana processes in 60 to 75 days end to end. Rajasthan and Madhya Pradesh 90 to 130 days. Maharashtra and Uttar Pradesh 115 to 170 days. If your application is stuck at the state nodal agency for more than 45 days without a status update, escalate to the state renewable energy agency office or file a grievance at the MNRE PM-KUSUM portal.

**One update most competitor pages have not covered yet**

The MNRE memorandum of 28 March 2026 extended the Phase 1 completion deadline to 31 March 2027 for any project with a Power Purchase Agreement or Notice to Proceed issued on or before 31 December 2025. And Union Budget 2026-27 announced PM-KUSUM 2.0 with a Rs 50,000 crore Phase 2 outlay over the next 5 years and a fresh 10 GW agrivoltaics component. So the scheme is not ending in March 2026 as some older articles still claim.

I wrote a longer breakdown with HP-wise farmer share tables and state completion leaderboards if you want the full reference: https://www.sarkaridarapan.com/sarkari-yojana/pm-kusum-yojana-solar-pump-for-farmers-2026

Primary sources: mnre.gov.in PM-KUSUM page, pmkusum.mnre.gov.in national dashboard, and the state nodal agency portals listed above.
