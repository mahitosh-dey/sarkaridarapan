## Draft

At 25, APY is one of the cheapest guaranteed-pension products you can buy in India, but only if you meet the eligibility rule that changed in October 2022. If you paid income tax in any of the last three assessment years, you can't join APY anymore. That rule was notified in the Ministry of Finance gazette on 10 August 2022 and took effect from 1 October 2022.

Assuming you're not an income-tax payer, the maths at 25 is genuinely attractive.

Monthly contribution at age 25 for a guaranteed Rs. 5,000 pension after 60:  **Rs. 376**
For Rs. 3,000 pension:  **Rs. 226**
For Rs. 1,000 pension:  **Rs. 76**

So Rs. 376 every month for 35 years buys a Rs. 5,000 monthly pension for life, plus the same pension continues for your spouse after your death, plus the accumulated corpus (approx Rs. 8.5 lakh at age 60 based on 8% return) is paid to your nominee when both of you pass. That's a triple benefit for less than the price of one Zomato order a month.

The scheme was extended till FY 2030-31 by the Union Cabinet on 25 October 2024, so enrolment stays open at least till 2031.

Where APY loses to alternatives:

- **NPS Tier-I** gives you Rs. 50,000 extra 80CCD(1B) deduction plus 60% tax-free withdrawal at 60. At 25, if you can afford Rs. 5,000/month or more, NPS builds a bigger corpus than APY through market-linked equity exposure. But NPS pension amount isn't guaranteed.
- **PPF** compounds at ~7.1% (Q2 FY 2026-27), fully tax-free, but there's no lifelong monthly pension. It's a lump-sum at maturity.
- **Mutual fund SIP** in a large-cap fund can historically compound at 11-13% CAGR over 35 years, but you carry market risk and there's no guaranteed floor.

Best real-world combination for a 25-year-old who's not an income-tax payer: APY for the guaranteed floor (Rs. 376/month), plus a Rs. 2,000-3,000/month equity SIP for growth. APY covers the "worst case", the SIP builds the "good case".

If you're already an income-tax payer, skip APY entirely and split between NPS Tier-I (for 80CCD deduction) and equity SIP.

I've written a fuller explainer with the 2026 contribution chart, penal-interest rules, and the exit rules if you want to leave APY early: [Atal Pension Yojana 2026 guide](https://www.sarkaridarapan.com/sarkari-yojana/atal-pension-yojana-apy-2026).
